Follow-ups:

Q: What is the difference between a senior engineer and a lead engineer?

> A senior engineer can solve difficult problems well. A lead engineer also shapes how the team solves problems: architecture, standards, trade-offs, mentoring, delivery sequencing, and alignment with stakeholders. The lead engineer should make the team stronger, not just personally ship more code.

Q: How would you mentor others?

> I would combine pairing, design reviews, lightweight written patterns, and clear examples. For AI engineering, I would focus on habits like defining evals before demos, logging enough to debug, designing human-in-the-loop points, keeping prompts and tools versioned, and documenting known failure modes.

Q: How do you handle disagreement with another engineer?

> I try to move the discussion from preference to evidence. I would clarify the requirement, agree decision criteria, compare trade-offs, and where possible run a small experiment. If the decision has risk or architecture implications, I would bring in the right reviewers early rather than letting disagreement become personal or late-stage.
