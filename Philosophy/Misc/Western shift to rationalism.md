The turn towards scientific rationalism, while tremendously fruitful in expanding our knowledge of the physical universe, also came at a great cost - the diminishing of other vital ways of knowing and experiencing reality. The positivist insistence that only empirically verifiable claims constitute real knowledge seems to close off dimensions of human experience and inquiry outside the material realm.

The mystical insights, metaphysical frameworks, and holistic perspectives offered by ancient philosophers, spiritual traditions, and other non-Western worldviews should not be so easily discarded or diminished as mere superstition or irrationality. They speak to profound strata of human existence - consciousness, meaning, values, interconnectedness - that the scientific method alone cannot fully illuminate.

Figures like Plato and Aristotle developed extraordinarily rich metaphysical systems attempting to reconcile the ideal and material realms. While flawed in some respects, their traditions should not be callously brushed aside simply because they don't conform to modern empirical standards. There are modes of experiential and philosophical knowledge that transcend the solely materialistic.

One counter-objection from secularists or empiricists may be: 
	Critique of metaphysical knowledge claims: A key criticism from secular rationalists is that metaphysical and religious truth claims ultimately lack empirical grounding or intersubjective verifiability. Things like spiritual experiences or revealed scriptures are subjective and lack the rigor of the scientific method. Thus, integrating metaphysics undermines claims to universality.

However, one further response to this is regarding how certain findings and principles from quantum physics seem to challenge the strict materialist/empiricist dismissal of metaphysical knowledge claims.

The famous double-slit experiment, which shows how particles can exhibit characteristics of both particles and waves depending on whether they are observed or not, points to the fundamental role that consciousness/observation plays in shaping reality at the quantum level.

Phenomena like quantum entanglement, where particles can instantaneously influence each other over vast distances in a way that defies our conventional understanding of space and time, also hint at deeper interconnected layers of existence beyond just the material realm.

Leading physicists like Niels Bohr emphasized how quantum theory requires us to abandon classical assumptions about objective reality existing independently of observation/measurement. The mere act of observing affects the outcome on the quantum scale.

So in that sense, quantum physics has opened up a Pandora's box of empirical findings that seem to defy pure materialism and determinism. Consciousness and observation appear intimately tied to the most fundamental workings of reality itself.

This poses a direct challenge to the empiricist/positivist rejection of metaphysics or non-material causation as being unverifiable or supernatural "mumbo-jumbo." The quantum world provides quantitative, empirically-verified examples where observation/consciousness undeniably shapes physical reality in counter-intuitive ways.

You could argue this gives credence to more expansive metaphysical frameworks that see reality as fundamentally interconnected, or acknowledge consciousness as having deeper ontological significance beyond just epiphenomenal material effects.

So from the perspective of metaphysicians or theorists of consciousness, quantum physics provides tantalizing empirical grounding for metaphysical principles they have long espoused. It undermines the strict distinctions between the physical/non-physical that materialists insist upon.

Of course, interpretations of quantum mechanics are still hotly debated among physicists. But findings from this realm have undeniably challenged the hegemony of reductive, observer-independent materialist assumptions that dismissively rejected all metaphysical insights as baseless speculation.

The strangeness of the quantum universe provides an open door for reconciling metaphysical views and modern physics in potentially revolutionary ways. It gives pause to those who would too quickly disregard all metaphysical inquiry as fundamentally irreconcilable with science and empiricism.
