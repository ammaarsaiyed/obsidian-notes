# The First Fortress
**From the book *Mufarrij al-Kurub wa Mufarrih al-Qulub* (The Reliever of Distresses and the Gladdener of Hearts)**

---

### (1) Supplication
In the name of God (Allah), The Compassionate (Al-Rahman), The Merciful (Al-Rahim), and there is no power nor strength except by God (Allah), The Most High (Al-Ali), The Great (Al-Azim).

**Reference/Benefit:**
From Ali, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, he said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said to me: "O Ali, shall I not teach you words that if you fall into a predicament you say them?" I said: "Yes, may God (Allah) make me your ransom." He said: "If you fall into a predicament, say: In the name of God (Allah), The Most Compassionate (ar-Rahman), The Most Merciful (ar-Rahim), and there is no power nor strength except by God (Allah), The Most High (al-Ali), The Supreme (al-Azim). For indeed, God (Allah), The Exalted (Ta'ala), turns away by them whatever He wills from the types of affliction."

---

### (2) Supplication
O God (Allah), I ask You by the fact that to You belongs all praise, there is no deity but You, the Originator (Badi') of the heavens and the earth, O Possessor of Majesty and Honor (Dha Al-Jalal wa Al-Ikram), O Living (Hayy), O Sustainer (Qayyum).

**Reference/Benefit:**
Al-Hakim recorded from Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: We were with the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, and a man was standing praying. When he bowed, prostrated, recited the Tashahhud, and supplicated, he said in his supplication: "O God (Allahumma), I ask You by the fact that to You belongs the praise" up to his saying: "O Ever-Living (Ya Hayy), O Sustainer of all existence (Ya Qayyum)." The Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "He has supplicated to God (Allah) by His Greatest Name, which if He is called by it He answers, and if He is asked by it He gives."

---

### (3) Supplication
O God (Allah), I ask You by the fact that to You belongs all praise, there is no deity but You, The Most Compassionate (Al-Hannan), The Bestower (Al-Mannan), the Originator (Badi') of the heavens and the earth, O Possessor of Majesty and Honor (Dha Al-Jalal wa Al-Ikram), I ask You for Paradise and I seek refuge in You from the Fire.

**Reference/Benefit:**
An-Nasa'i recorded from Ibn Mas'ud, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, used to supplicate: "O God (Allahumma), I ask You by Your Name which if He is called by it He answers, and if He is asked by it He gives."

---

### (4) Supplication
O God (Allah), I ask You by [the truth that] there is no deity but You, the Lord (Rabb) of the seven heavens and the Lord (Rabb) of the Great Throne, and I ask You by [the truth that] there is no deity but You, the Lord (Rabb) of the seven heavens and the Lord (Rabb) of the Noble Throne, and I ask You by [the truth that] there is no deity but You, the Lord (Rabb) of the seven heavens and the seven earths and whatever is in them, indeed You are Capable (Qadir) over all things. (Then one asks God for their need).

**Reference/Benefit:**
Al-Bukhari recorded in Al-Adab Al-Mufrad from Ibn Abbas, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, who said: "Whoever is befallen by distress, or sorrow, or anguish, or fears a ruler, and supplicates with these words, he will be answered: O God (Allahumma), I ask You by there is no deity but You" up to his saying: "Indeed, You are over all things competent (Qadir)." And it is apparent that Ibn Abbas narrates it from the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him.

---

### (5) Supplication
O Lord (Ya Rabb), O Lord (Ya Rabb), O Lord (Ya Rabb), O Lord (Ya Rabb).

**Reference/Benefit:**
Al-Bazzar and Abu Ash-Shaikh recorded in *Ath-Thawab* from Aisha, may God (Allah) be pleased with her, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "If the servant says 'O Lord (Ya Rabb), O Lord (Ya Rabb)' four times, God (Allah) says: 'At your service, My servant, ask and you shall be given.'" And from Jabir similar to it, narrated by Ad-Daylami.

---

### (6) Supplication
O Originator (Ya Badi') of the heavens, O Living (Ya Hayy), O Sustainer (Ya Qayyum).

**Reference/Benefit:**
Al-Bukhari recorded in Al-Adab Al-Mufrad from Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: I was with the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, and a man supplicated and said: "O Originator of the heavens (Ya Badi' as-Samawat), O Ever-Living (Ya Hayy), O Sustainer of all existence (Ya Qayyum), I ask You." The Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "Do you know by what he supplicated? By the One in whose hand is my soul, he has supplicated to God (Allah) by His Name which if He is called by it, He answers."

---

### (7) Supplication
O God (Allah), I ask You from all goodness, what I know of it and what I do not know, and I ask You by Your Great Name by which if You are called upon, You answer, and if You are asked by it, You give.

**Reference/Benefit:**
At-Tabarani recorded in Al-Awsat from Anas that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, entered upon Aisha, may God (Allah) be pleased with her, one morning and she said: "O Messenger of God (Allah), teach me the Greatest Name of God (Allah) which if He is called by it He answers, and if He is asked by it He gives." So he turned his face away. She stood, performed ablution, and said: "O God (Allahumma), I ask You for all good" up to her saying: "and if You are asked by it, You give." He said: "By God (Allah), it is indeed within these names."

---

### (8) Supplication
There is no deity but God (Allah), and God (Allah) is the Greatest (Akbar), there is no deity but God (Allah) alone, there is no deity but God (Allah) alone with no partner to Him, to Him belongs the dominion and to Him belongs all praise and He is Capable (Qadir) over all things, there is no deity but God (Allah) and there is no power nor strength except by God (Allah).

**Reference/Benefit:**
At-Tabarani recorded in Al-Kabir and Al-Awsat with a good chain from Mu'awiyah bin Abi Sufyan, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, who said: I heard the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, saying: "Whoever supplicates with these five words will not ask God (Allah) for anything except that He will give it to him: There is no deity but God (Allah) and God (Allah) is the greatest" up to his saying: "and there is no power nor strength except by God (Allah)."

---

### (9) Supplication
O God (Allah), I am Your servant, the son of Your male servant, the son of Your female servant, my forelock is in Your hand, Your command over me is passing, Your decree over me is just. I ask You by every name that is Yours, by which You named Yourself, or revealed in Your book, or taught to anyone from Your creation, or kept exclusively in the knowledge of the unseen with You, that You make the Great Quran the spring of my heart, the light of my chest, the remover of my sadness, and the departure of my anxiety.

**Reference/Benefit:**
Imam Ahmad, Ibn Hibban, Al-Hakim, and Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Ibn Mas'ud, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "No servant is afflicted by distress or sorrow and says: 'O God (Allahumma), I am Your servant' up to his saying: 'and a departure for my distress' except that God (Allah) takes away his distress and replaces his sorrow with relief." They said: "O Messenger of God (Allah), should we not learn these words?" He said: "Yes, it behoves whoever hears them to learn them."

---

### (10) Supplication
Sufficient for me is the Lord (Al-Rabb) over the servants, sufficient for me is the Creator (Al-Khaliq) over the created beings, sufficient for me is the Provider (Al-Razzaq) over those who are provided for, sufficient for me is He who is my sufficiency, sufficient for me is God (Allah) and He is the excellent Disposer of affairs (Al-Wakil), sufficient for me is God (Allah), there is no deity but Him, upon Him I have relied, and He is the Lord (Rabb) of the Great Throne.

**Reference/Benefit:**
Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from the route of Al-Khalil from the jurist of the people of Jordan who said: It reached us that the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, whenever he was afflicted by sorrow or anguish, used to say: "Sufficient for me is The Lord (ar-Rabb) from the servants" up to his saying: "and He is The Lord of the Great Throne (Rabb al-Arsh al-Azim)."

---

### (11) Supplication
I have relied upon the Living (Al-Hayy) who does not die, and praise be to God (Allah) who has not taken a son, and has no partner in the dominion, and does not have a protecting friend (Wali) out of weakness, and magnify Him with all magnification.

**Reference/Benefit:**
Ibn As-Sunni and Al-Hakim recorded from Abu Hurairah, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "No matter distressed me except that Jibril appeared to me and said: O Muhammad, say: I rely upon The Ever-Living (al-Hayy) who does not die, to the end of the verse." And Al-Hakim and At-Tabarani narrated it from Abu Hurairah.

---

### (12) Supplication
Sufficient for me is God (Allah) and the excellent Disposer of affairs (Al-Wakil).

**Reference/Benefit:**
From Shaddad bin Aws, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "God is sufficient for me, and He is the best disposer of affairs (Hasbi Allah wa Ni'm al-Wakil) is a safety for every fearful person." And Imam Ja'far bin Muhammad, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, said: I marvel at one who fears harm, how does it escape him to say: 'God is sufficient for us, and He is the best disposer of affairs (Hasbuna Allah wa Ni'm al-Wakil),' while God (Allah), The Exalted (Ta'ala), says: {So they returned with grace from God (Allah) and bounty, no harm having touched them}. And I marvel at one who is afflicted with sorrow, how does it escape him to say: 'There is no deity but You, glory be to You, indeed I have been of the wrongdoers,' while God (Allah), The Glorified (Subhanahu), says: {So We answered him and saved him from the distress, and thus do We save the believers}. Al-Halabi said it.

---

### (13) Supplication
Sufficient for us is God (Allah) and the excellent Disposer of affairs (Al-Wakil).

**Reference/Benefit:**
Ibn Mardawayh recorded from Abu Hurairah, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that he said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "If you fall into a great matter, then say: God is sufficient for us, and He is the best disposer of affairs (Hasbuna Allah wa Ni'm al-Wakil)."

---

### (14) Supplication
Sufficient for me is God (Allah) for such and such, sufficient for me is God (Allah) for what concerns me, sufficient for me is God (Allah) against whoever oppresses me, sufficient for me is God (Allah) against whoever plots evil against me.

**Reference/Benefit:**
Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Al-Baqir Muhammad bin Ali that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, taught Ali, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, a supplication to supplicate with for everything that distressed him, and Ali used to teach it to his son: "O Existing One before everything" up to his saying: "do with me such and such."

---

### (15) Supplication
You were and You will be, and You are Living (Hayy) and do not die; eyes sleep and stars dim, while You are Living (Hayy), Sustaining (Qayyum), neither slumber nor sleep overtakes You, O Living (Ya Hayy), O Sustainer (Ya Qayyum).

**Reference/Benefit:**
Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Ad-Dahhak who said: The supplication of Musa, peace be upon him, when he headed towards Pharaoh, and the supplication of the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, on the day of Hunayn, and the supplication of every distressed person is this: "You were and You will be" up to his saying: "O Ever-Living (Ya Hayy), O Sustainer of all existence (Ya Qayyum)."

---

### (16) Supplication
There is no deity but God (Allah), The Great (Al-Azim), The Forbearing (Al-Halim), there is no deity but God (Allah), Lord (Rabb) of the Great Throne, there is no deity but God (Allah), Lord (Rabb) of the heavens, Lord (Rabb) of the earth, Lord (Rabb) of the Noble Throne.

**Reference/Benefit:**
The two Sheikhs (Al-Bukhari and Muslim) recorded in their Sahihs from Ibn Abbas, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, that the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, used to say during distress: "There is no deity but God (Allah), The Supreme (al-Azim), The Forbearing (al-Halim)" up to his saying: "The Lord of the Noble Throne (Rabb al-Arsh al-Karim)." Imam An-Nawawi said in the explanation of Muslim in the chapter of Remembrance during distress and grave matters: At-Tabari said: The predecessors used to supplicate with it and call it the supplication of distress. If it is said this is praise and there is no supplication in it, he answered it from two angles: One of them is that this remembrance is used to open the supplication, then he supplicates with what he wills. And the second is the answer of Sufyan bin Uyaynah, and the poet said: *If a man praises you one day, the praise suffices him from having to explicitly present his request.* End of the speech of Imam An-Nawawi.

---

### (17) Supplication
There is no deity but God (Allah), The Forbearing (Al-Halim), The Generous (Al-Karim), Glory be to God (Allah), Lord (Rabb) of the seven heavens and Lord (Rabb) of the Great Throne, there is no deity but You, mighty is Your protection, and glorious is Your praise, and there is no deity other than You.

**Reference/Benefit:**
In *Hall al-Iqal*, this supplication is mentioned from Ibn Abbas, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "O Ever-Living (Ya Hayy), O Forbearing (Ya Halim)" up to his saying "There is no deity but You." He mentioned it like this with truncated wording, and perhaps its deficiency in *Hall al-Iqal* is from the copyist.

---

### (18) Supplication
There is no deity but God (Allah), The Most High (Al-Ali), The Forbearing (Al-Halim), there is no deity but God (Allah), Lord (Rabb) of the Great Throne, there is no deity but God (Allah), Lord (Rabb) of the seven heavens and Lord (Rabb) of the Noble Throne.

**Reference/Benefit:**
Imam Al-Ghazali said in *Al-Ihya*: If distress afflicts you, say: "There is no deity but God (Allah), The Most High (al-Ali), The Forbearing (al-Halim), there is no deity but God (Allah), The Lord of the Great Throne (Rabb al-Arsh al-Azim), there is no deity but God (Allah), The Lord of the seven heavens and The Lord of the Noble Throne (Rabb as-Samawati as-Sab' wa Rabb al-Arsh al-Karim)." He said he extracted it from the hadith of Ibn Abbas, and Az-Zubaydi mentioned some of the wordings: so whoever wishes to view that, he may, and its mention will come in the upcoming Fortresses with slight variations in some wordings from other narrations.

---

### (19) Supplication
O God (Allah), to You I complain of the weakness of my strength, the lack of my stratagem, and my insignificance before people. O Most Merciful of the merciful (Ya Arham Al-Rahimin), You are the Lord (Rabb) of the weak, and You are my Lord (Rabb), to whom do You leave me? To a distant person who receives me with hostility, or to an enemy to whom You have given control over my affair? If there is no anger from You upon me, then I do not care, except that Your granting of well-being is more expansive for me. I seek refuge in the light of Your Face by which darknesses are illuminated and upon which the affairs of this world and the Hereafter are set right, lest Your anger descends upon me or Your wrath settles upon me. To You is the right to reproach until You are pleased, and there is no power nor strength except by God (Allah).

**Reference/Benefit:**
When the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, went out to Ta'if on his feet and called them to Islam but they did not answer him, he departed and came to the shade of a tree, prayed two units of prayer, then said: "O God (Allahumma), to You I complain" up to his saying: "and there is no power nor strength except by You." Ibn Ad-Durayhim Al-Mawsili mentioned it in his book *Ghayat Al-Maghnam*, and the authors of the Sira have also mentioned it.

---

### (20) Supplication
O Most Merciful of the merciful (Ya Arham Al-Rahimin) (Three times).

**Reference/Benefit:**
From Abu Umamah, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "Indeed, God (Allah) has an angel entrusted with whoever says: O Most Merciful of the merciful (Ya Arham ar-Rahimin). So whoever says it three times, the angel says to him: The Most Merciful of the merciful (Arham ar-Rahimin) has turned His attention to you, so ask for your need." And Al-Hakim recorded from Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, passed by a man while he was saying: "O Most Merciful of the merciful (Ya Arham ar-Rahimin)." So he said to him: "Ask, for God (Allah) has looked at you."

---

### (21) Supplication
O God (Allah), I ask You and I turn to You by Your Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings of God be upon him, the Prophet of mercy. O our master, O Muhammad, I turn by you to my Lord (Rabb) concerning my need so that it may be fulfilled. O God (Allah), accept his intercession for me.

**Reference/Benefit:**
From Uthman bin Hunaif, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that he was with the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, when a blind man came to him and complained to him about the loss of his sight and said: "O Messenger of God (Allah), I have no guide and it has become difficult for me." So the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said to him: "Go to the ablution place, perform ablution, then pray two units of prayer, then say: O God (Allahumma), I ask You" up to his saying: "O God (Allahumma), accept his intercession for me." Uthman said: By God (Allah), we did not part, nor did our conversation prolong, until the man entered and it was as if there had never been any affliction in him. Abu Dawud, At-Tirmidhi, and others narrated it, and Al-Hakim authenticated it in his *Mustadrak*.

---

### (22) Supplication
O Light (Ya Nur) of the heavens and the earth, O Sustainer (Ya Qayyum) of the heavens and the earth, O Pillar (Ya Imad) of the heavens and the earth, O Adornment (Ya Zayn) of the heavens and the earth, O Beauty (Ya Jamal) of the heavens and the earth, O Originator (Ya Badi') of the heavens and the earth, O Possessor of Majesty and Honor (Ya Dha Al-Jalal wa Al-Ikram), O Savior of those who seek salvation (Ya Ghawth Al-Mustaghithin), and the ultimate goal of the desirers, and the Reliever of the distressed, and the Gladdener of the grief-stricken, and the Answerer of the cries of those who cry out, and the Responder to the supplication of the desperate, and the Remover of great distress, O God (Ya Allah), O Compassionate (Ya Rahman), O Merciful (Ya Rahim), O Most Merciful of the merciful (Ya Arham Al-Rahimin), remove from me my grief, my anxiety, and my distress, and grant me my request.

**Reference/Benefit:**
Ibn Abi Hatim narrated in the book of Supplication from Musa bin Uqbah that Jibril, peace be upon him, said to the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him: "I have not been sent to anyone more beloved to me than you. Shall I not teach you a supplication I have chosen for you that I have not taught to anyone before you, to supplicate with in desire and fear: O Light of the heavens and the earth (Ya Nur as-samawati wal-ard)" up to his saying: "For indeed You see my condition and the condition of my companions." He said: So God (Allah) struck down his enemy, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him.

---

### (23) Supplication
*(And from what was reported from some of the Prophets, peace and blessings be upon them):*
O Possessor of favor that never ceases and which no one else can enumerate.

**Reference/Benefit:**
Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Yahya bin Sulaym that it reached him that the Angel of Death asked his Lord (Rabb) for permission to greet Yaqub (Jacob), peace be upon him, so He permitted him. He came to him and greeted him. He (Yaqub) said to him: "By the One who created you, have you taken the soul of Yusuf (Joseph)?" He said: "No." He said to him: "Shall I not teach you words by which you do not ask God (Allah) for anything except He gives it to you?" He said: "Yes." He said: "So say: O Possessor of continuous favor which never ceases and which none but Him can count (Ya Dha al-Ma'ruf alladhi la yanqati'u abadan wa la yuhsihi ghayruh)." He said: So dawn did not break until he was brought the shirt of Yusuf.

---

### (24) Supplication
God (Allah) is my Lord (Rabb) and I do not associate anything with Him.

**Reference/Benefit:**
Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Ibrahim bin Khallad who said: Jibril descended upon Yaqub, peace be upon them both, so he complained to him about what he was in. He said: "Shall I not teach you a supplication that if you supplicate with it, God (Allah) will bring relief to you?" He said: "Yes." He said: "Say: O He whom no one knows how He is except Him" up to his saying: "bring relief to me." So the bearer of good news came to him.

---

### (25) Supplication
O He who no one knows how He is except Him, and O He whose power no one else can reach, relieve me.

**Reference/Benefit:**
Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Muhammad bin Umar from a man of the people of Kufa that Jibril entered upon Yusuf, peace be upon him, in the prison and said: "Say: O God (Allahumma), O Witness who is not absent (Ya Shahidan ghayra gha'ib)" up to his saying: "and provide for me from where I do not expect." As-Suyuti said it. And Ibn Ad-Durayhim mentioned in *Ghayat Al-Maghnam* from Al-Khattab bin Uthman with his chain. And Al-Khara'iti recorded in *Makarim Al-Akhlaq* from Abdullah bin Alqamah At-Ta'i that Jibril came to Yusuf, peace be upon them both, in the prison and said: "I have been informed to teach you words; perhaps God (Allah) will benefit you by them. Say: O God (Allahumma), make for me from every distress that distresses me: a relief and a way out, and provide for me from where I do not expect."

---

### (26) Supplication
O God (Allah), I ask You by Your Name, The One (Al-Wahid), The Unique (Al-Ahad), and I supplicate to You by Your Name, The Absolute (Al-Samad), and I supplicate to You by Your Great (Al-Azim), Single (Al-Witr) Name which filled all the pillars, that You relieve me from what I have entered the evening in and what I have entered the morning in.

**Reference/Benefit:**
Al-Khatib recorded with a chain containing unknown narrators from Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, as elevated (to the Prophet) that when God (Allah) wanted to raise Isa (Jesus) to heaven, God (Allah) dictated to him a supplication, so Isa, peace be upon him, supplicated with it, which is: "Say: O God (Allahumma), I ask You by Your Name, The One (al-Wahid), The Unique (al-Ahad)" up to his saying: "and what I have entered the morning in." So Isa, peace be upon him, supplicated with it, and God (Allah), The Mighty and Majestic (Azza wa Jall), revealed to Jibril, peace be upon him, to: "Raise My servant to Me."

---

### (27) Supplication
O God (Allah), just as You were gentle in Your greatness beyond the gentle ones, and You were exalted in Your greatness over the great ones, and You knew what is beneath Your earth just as You know what is above Your Throne, and the whispers of the chests were like public declarations to You, and the public declaration of speech was like a secret in Your knowledge, and everything submitted to Your greatness, and every possessor of authority yielded to Your authority, and the affair of the world and the Hereafter all became in Your hand, make for me from every anxiety I entered the evening in a relief and a way out. O God (Allah), indeed Your pardon of my sins, Your overlooking of my mistakes, and Your concealing of my ugly deeds has made me hopeful to ask You for what I do not deserve from You regarding that in which I fell short. I supplicate to You feeling secure, and I ask You feeling intimate, and You are the Benefactor to me while I am the wrongdoer to myself in what is between me and You. You show love to me with blessings and I make myself hateful to You with sins, but trust in You has carried me to be bold toward You, so return with Your bounty and goodness upon me, indeed You are The Acceptor of Repentance (Al-Tawwab), The Merciful (Al-Rahim).

**Reference/Benefit:**
This supplication, "O God (Allahumma), just as You were gentle..." to its end, was mentioned by Imam Al-Ghazali in *Al-Ihya* in the book of Enjoining Good, and he mentioned a story for it, the summary of which is that Abu Ja'far Al-Mansur, while he was circumambulating at night, heard a man saying: "O God (Allahumma), I complain to You of the appearance of transgression and corruption" in a speech. So he ordered him to be brought. The man was brought to him and confronted the man (Al-Mansur) by mentioning his injustices and admonished him severely. Al-Mansur wept, then later asked about the man but could not find him. They searched for him and one of his inner circle found him. He said: "I am not going with you." He replied: "If you do not go with me, he will kill me." He said to him: "He is not capable of that," and took out a parchment for him containing this supplication. He said: "Take it and put it in your pocket, for in it is the supplication of relief." He said: "And what is the supplication of relief?" He said: "None is granted it except the martyrs. Whoever supplicates with it evening and morning, his sins are demolished, his joy lasts, his mistakes are erased, his supplication is answered, his provision is expanded, his hope is given, he is aided against his enemy, he is written with God (Allah) as a truthful one, and he does not die except as a martyr. You say: O God (Allahumma), just as You were gentle in Your greatness beyond the gentle ones" to the end of the supplication. He said: So I took it and put it in my pocket, then I had no concern other than the Commander of the Faithful. I entered and greeted him. He raised his head, looked at me, smiled, then said: "Woe to you, do you practice magic?" I said: "No, by God (Allah)." Then I told him my story with the old man. He said: "Bring the parchment he gave you," and he ordered it to be copied, and gave me ten thousand dirhams. Then he said: "Do you know him?" I said: "No." He said: "That was Al-Khidr, peace be upon him." End of the summary from *Al-Ihya*. And he mentioned the story in *Al-Manhaj Al-Hanif* and the supplication, and added at its end: "For indeed You said, and Your saying is the truth: God (Allah) is Subtle (Latif) with His servants, He provides for whom He wills, and He is The Strong (al-Qawi), The Exalted in Might (al-Aziz). O Subtle/Gentle (Ya Latif), O All-Aware (Ya Khabir), O Preserver (Ya Hafiz)." ### (39) & (40) Supplication
(39) O God (Allah), send blessings upon our master Muhammad, the luminous tree of origin, the flash of the Merciful handful, the best of human creation, the most honorable of bodily forms, the source of divine secrets, and the treasuries of chosen sciences; the possessor of the original handful, the radiant splendor, and the elevated rank; under whose banner the prophets are enlisted, for they are from him and return to him. **Reference/Benefit:**
These two noble prayers (on the Prophet) belong to the Pole of the Poles, Sidi Ahmad Al-Badawi, may God (Allah) benefit us by him. As for the first prayer... it is read after every prayer seven times... equivalent to thirty-three of *Dala'il Al-Khayrat*. ...cause for attaining many lights and the unveiling of many secrets... facilitating outward provision... and inward provision... victory is attained over the self, Satan, and all enemies. Whoever persists in reading it every day a hundred times... for forty days with uprightness, will attain from the lights and good what none knows its measure except God (Allah).
As for the second summarized prayer... attributed to our master... Sayyid Ahmad Al-Badawi... tested and proven for fulfilling needs, relieving distresses, repelling dilemmas, and attaining lights and secrets... The count of its litany is a hundred times every day.

### (41) The Highest Role (Al-Dawr Al-A'la)
**By my master Muhyi al-Din Ibn Arabi, may God be pleased with him**

In the name of God (Allah), The Compassionate (Al-Rahman), The Merciful (Al-Rahim). O God (Allah), O Living (Ya Hayy), O Sustainer (Ya Qayyum), in You I have sought fortification, so protect me with the protection of sufficiency, guarding, and a sanctuary of safety. In the name of God (Allah) (Whatever God wills, there is no power except by God) I have entered into the protection of God (Allah) and I have sought asylum with the Messenger of God, peace and blessings of God be upon him. O God (Allah), O Most High (Ya Ali), O Great (Ya Azim), make the veil of preservation and salvation shine for me, (and hold firmly to the rope of God all together). O God (Ya Allah), O All-Encompassing (Ya Muhit), O Capable (Ya Qadir), wall me with a wall of safety, encompassing, realm of dominion (Malakut), might, and greatness (that is better, that is from the signs of God). And give me refuge O Watchful (Ya Raqib), O Responder (Ya Mujib), and guard me in myself, my religion, my family, my wealth, my children, and my home with the guardianship of giving refuge and rescue (and it will not harm them in anything except by the permission of God). And protect me O Preventer (Ya Mani'), O Defender (Ya Dafi'), by the right of Your Names, Your signs, and Your words, from the evil of the devil and the ruler, for if anyone from Your creation transgresses against me, fearing the punishment of God, then save me from their evil (O He in whose hand is the dominion of everything and He protects while none can protect against Him). And assist me against them and their helpers (with that by which the prophets and messengers overcame), (for God has forsaken him) and sealed his hearing and his heart and placed a covering over his vision, so who will guide him after God? And suffice me O Withholder (Ya Qabid), O Subduer (Ya Qahhar), against the deception of their plotting, and turn them away from me condemned and defeated (so they had no group to help them besides God). And make me taste O Supremely Pure (Ya Subbuh), O Most Holy (Ya Quddus) the sweetness of intimate discourse (approach and do not fear, indeed you are among the secure ones in Our sanctuary). And suffice me O Sufficient One (Ya Kafi) the evil of what I fear and dread (indeed We have sufficed you against the mockers) (so God will suffice you against them, and He is The All-Hearing, The All-Knowing), by the right of Ta-Ha, Ya-Sin, Qaf, and the Glorious Quran.

And there is no power nor strength except by God (Allah), by the verse (the remainder of God is better for you if you are believers). And protect me from the evil of what I fear and dread by the perfect words of God. And clothe me O Mighty (Ya Aziz), O Honorer (Ya Mu'izz), with the robe of reverence, pride, majesty, authority, dominion, and might (and do not let their speech grieve you, indeed all might belongs to God entirely). And clothe me O Majestic (Ya Jalil), O Great (Ya Kabir), with the robe of majesty, beauty, perfection, and acceptance (so when they saw him, they greatly admired him and cut their hands and said, God forbid). And cast upon me O Mighty (Ya Aziz), O Loving (Ya Wadud), a love from You by which You unite the hearts of Your servants (they love them as they love God, but those who believe are stronger in love for God). And manifest upon me O Manifest (Ya Zahir), O Hidden (Ya Batin), the traces, secrets, and lights of (He loves them and they love Him, humble toward the believers, stern toward the disbelievers, striving in the path of God). And direct, O God (Allah), my face with the purity, beauty, and radiance of (so if they turn away, say, sufficient for me is God, there is no deity but Him, upon Him I have relied and He is the Lord of the Great Throne). And beautify me O Originator (Ya Badi') of the heavens and the earth, O Possessor of Majesty and Honor (Ya Dha Al-Jalal wa Al-Ikram), with a tongue of eloquence and rhetoric (and untie the knot from my tongue so they may understand my speech), with the tenderness of compassion (then their skins and their hearts soften to the remembrance of God). And gird me O Severe in Grasp (Ya Shadida Al-Batsh), O Compeller (Ya Jabbar), with the sword of awe, severity, and invincibility from the might of Your compulsion (and victory is not but from God). And perfect upon me O Expander (Ya Basit), O Opener (Ya Fattah), the joy of delight (My Lord, expand for me my breast and ease for me my task) with the subtleties of recognitions (Did We not expand for you your breast?) and the glad tidings of that day (on that day the believers will rejoice in the victory of God). And descend, O God (Allah), O Gentle (Ya Latif), O Clement (Ya Ra'uf), into my heart faith, tranquility, calmness, and dignity so I may be among (those who believe and whose hearts find tranquility in the remembrance of God). And pour upon me O Patient (Ya Sabur), O Appreciative (Ya Shakur), patience and steadfastness (make our feet firm and grant us victory over the disbelieving people) (how many a small company has overcome a large company by the permission of God, and God is with the patient). And guard me O Preserver (Ya Hafiz), O Watchful (Ya Raqib), from before me, from behind me, from my right, from my left, from above me, and from below me (for him are successive angels before him and behind him, protecting him by the command of God) (and God, from behind them, is Encompassing; rather, it is a glorious Quran, in a preserved slate). And make firm, O God (Allah), my feet so I do not slip or be harmed (and be firm and remember God much so that you may succeed). And grant me victory O Excellent Patron (Ya Ni'ma Al-Mawla) and O Excellent Helper (Ya Ni'ma Al-Nasir) over my enemies, the victory of him to whom it was said (grant victory) and he said (I seek refuge in God from being among the ignorant). O Seeker (Ya Talib), O Overcomer (Ya Ghalib) (God has written: I will surely overcome, I and My messengers; indeed God is Strong, Mighty) and with reverence (Indeed, We have sent you as a witness and a bringer of good tidings and a warner, that you may believe in God and His Messenger, and honor him, and revere him). And suffice me O Sufficer (Ya Kafi) of evils, O Healer (Ya Shafi) of ailments, with the benefits of (If We had sent down this Quran upon a mountain, you would have seen it humbled and cleaving asunder from the fear of God). And bestow favor upon me O Bestower (Ya Wahhab), O Provider (Ya Razzaq), with attainment, arrival, acceptance, facilitation, and arrangement (eat and drink from the provision of God). And inspire me with the word of piety as You inspired Your beloved Muhammad, peace and blessings of God be upon him, when You said (so know that there is no deity but God). And befriend me O Protecting Friend (Ya Wali), O Most High (Ya Ali) with friendship, honor, and forgiveness, with an increase of yielding, granting happiness, and supplying (that is better, that is from the bounty of God). And grant me sovereignty O Rich (Ya Ghani), O Generous (Ya Karim) with the best outcome, sovereignty, honor, and forgiveness (as You honored those who lower their voices in the presence of the Messenger of God). And accept my repentance O Acceptor of Repentance (Ya Tawwab), O Forbearing (Ya Halim) with a sincere repentance so I may be among (those who, when they commit an immorality or wrong themselves, remember God and seek forgiveness for their sins, and who forgives sins except God?). And seal for me O Compassionate (Ya Rahman), O Merciful (Ya Rahim) with the seal of those who are saved and hopeful (O My servants who have transgressed against themselves, do not despair of the mercy of God). And settle me O All-Hearing (Ya Sami'), O Near (Ya Qarib) in the gardens of Eden (their call therein will be, 'Glory be to You, O God,' and their greeting therein will be, 'Peace,' and the last of their call will be, 'Praise be to God, Lord of the worlds'). O God (Ya Allah), O God (Ya Allah), O God (Ya Allah), O Lord (Ya Rabb), O Living (Ya Hayy), O Sustainer (Ya Qayyum), O Possessor of Majesty and Honor (Ya Dha Al-Jalal wa Al-Ikram), O Most Merciful of the merciful (Ya Arham Al-Rahimin), I ask You by the sanctity of these Names, verses, and words, for a supporting authority, provision, a cool heart, abundant knowledge, righteous deeds, an illuminated grave, an easy reckoning, and a great kingdom in the highest paradise (Al-Firdaws). And may God send blessings upon our master Muhammad and upon his family and all his companions, and send abundant peace until the Day of Judgment, and praise be to God (Allah), Lord (Rabb) of the worlds.

**Reference/Benefit:**
*Ad-Dawr Al-A'la* by Sidi Muhyi Ad-Din Ibn Arabi... Among its tested properties: love and esteem in hearts, protection from the evil companion, salvation in travel, safety from the sting of the scorpion and snake, facilitating difficult childbirth, fulfilling needs in all dealings, protection from weapons and plague... invalidates magic, inherits the memorization of knowledge and the Quran... if read after Surat Al-Waqi'ah after Asr, provision is increased and poverty is banished... safe from earthly and heavenly afflictions... protected from all Satanic, Jinn, and human afflictions... especially for one who persists in reading it after the Fajr obligation; obedience is produced for him from the upper and lower worlds... It requires before beginning to read it to read Al-Fatihah and Ayat Al-Kursi once each, and the beginning of Surat Al-An'am: {Praise be to God (Allah) who created the heavens and the earth...}. And after concluding, he reads: {Did We not expand} three times, and sends blessings upon the Prophet three times.
