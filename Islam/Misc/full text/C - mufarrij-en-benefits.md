**Referencing of the Prophetic Remembrances and Supplications**

(1) From Ali, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, he said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said to me: "O Ali, shall I not teach you words that if you fall into a predicament you say them?" I said: "Yes, may God (Allah) make me your ransom." He said: "If you fall into a predicament, say: In the name of God (Allah), The Most Compassionate (ar-Rahman), The Most Merciful (ar-Rahim), and there is no power nor strength except by God (Allah), The Most High (al-Ali), The Supreme (al-Azim). For indeed, God (Allah), The Exalted (Ta'ala), turns away by them whatever He wills from the types of affliction."

(2) Al-Hakim recorded from Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: We were with the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, and a man was standing praying. When he bowed, prostrated, recited the Tashahhud, and supplicated, he said in his supplication: "O God (Allahumma), I ask You by the fact that to You belongs the praise" up to his saying: "O Ever-Living (Ya Hayy), O Sustainer of all existence (Ya Qayyum)." The Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "He has supplicated to God (Allah) by His Greatest Name, which if He is called by it He answers, and if He is asked by it He gives."

(3) An-Nasa'i recorded from Ibn Mas'ud, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, used to supplicate: "O God (Allahumma), I ask You by Your Name which if He is called by it He answers, and if He is asked by it He gives."

(4) Al-Bukhari recorded in Al-Adab Al-Mufrad from Ibn Abbas, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, who said: "Whoever is befallen by distress, or sorrow, or anguish, or fears a ruler, and supplicates with these words, he will be answered: O God (Allahumma), I ask You by there is no deity but You" up to his saying: "Indeed, You are over all things competent (Qadir)." And it is apparent that Ibn Abbas narrates it from the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him.

(5) Al-Bazzar and Abu Ash-Shaikh recorded in *Ath-Thawab* from Aisha, may God (Allah) be pleased with her, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "If the servant says 'O Lord (Ya Rabb), O Lord (Ya Rabb)' four times, God (Allah) says: 'At your service, My servant, ask and you shall be given.'" And from Jabir similar to it, narrated by Ad-Daylami.

(6) Al-Bukhari recorded in Al-Adab Al-Mufrad from Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: I was with the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, and a man supplicated and said: "O Originator of the heavens (Ya Badi' as-Samawat), O Ever-Living (Ya Hayy), O Sustainer of all existence (Ya Qayyum), I ask You." The Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "Do you know by what he supplicated? By the One in whose hand is my soul, he has supplicated to God (Allah) by His Name which if He is called by it, He answers."

(7) At-Tabarani recorded in Al-Awsat from Anas that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, entered upon Aisha, may God (Allah) be pleased with her, one morning and she said: "O Messenger of God (Allah), teach me the Greatest Name of God (Allah) which if He is called by it He answers, and if He is asked by it He gives." So he turned his face away. She stood, performed ablution, and said: "O God (Allahumma), I ask You for all good" up to her saying: "and if You are asked by it, You give." He said: "By God (Allah), it is indeed within these names."

(8) At-Tabarani recorded in Al-Kabir and Al-Awsat with a good chain from Mu'awiyah bin Abi Sufyan, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, who said: I heard the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, saying: "Whoever supplicates with these five words will not ask God (Allah) for anything except that He will give it to him: There is no deity but God (Allah) and God (Allah) is the greatest" up to his saying: "and there is no power nor strength except by God (Allah)."

(9) Imam Ahmad, Ibn Hibban, Al-Hakim, and Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Ibn Mas'ud, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "No servant is afflicted by distress or sorrow and says: 'O God (Allahumma), I am Your servant' up to his saying: 'and a departure for my distress' except that God (Allah) takes away his distress and replaces his sorrow with relief." They said: "O Messenger of God (Allah), should we not learn these words?" He said: "Yes, it behoves whoever hears them to learn them."

(10) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from the route of Al-Khalil from the jurist of the people of Jordan who said: It reached us that the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, whenever he was afflicted by sorrow or anguish, used to say: "Sufficient for me is The Lord (ar-Rabb) from the servants" up to his saying: "and He is The Lord of the Great Throne (Rabb al-Arsh al-Azim)."

(11) Ibn As-Sunni and Al-Hakim recorded from Abu Hurairah, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "No matter distressed me except that Jibril appeared to me and said: O Muhammad, say: I rely upon The Ever-Living (al-Hayy) who does not die, to the end of the verse." And Al-Hakim and At-Tabarani narrated it from Abu Hurairah.

(12) From Shaddad bin Aws, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "God is sufficient for me, and He is the best disposer of affairs (Hasbi Allah wa Ni'm al-Wakil) is a safety for every fearful person." And Imam Ja'far bin Muhammad, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, said: I marvel at one who fears harm, how does it escape him to say: 'God is sufficient for us, and He is the best disposer of affairs (Hasbuna Allah wa Ni'm al-Wakil),' while God (Allah), The Exalted (Ta'ala), says: *{So they returned with grace from God (Allah) and bounty, no harm having touched them}*. And I marvel at one who is afflicted with sorrow, how does it escape him to say: 'There is no deity but You, glory be to You, indeed I have been of the wrongdoers,' while God (Allah), The Glorified (Subhanahu), says: *{So We answered him and saved him from the distress, and thus do We save the believers}*. Al-Halabi said it.

(13) Ibn Mardawayh recorded from Abu Hurairah, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that he said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "If you fall into a great matter, then say: God is sufficient for us, and He is the best disposer of affairs (Hasbuna Allah wa Ni'm al-Wakil)."

(14) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Al-Baqir Muhammad bin Ali that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, taught Ali, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, a supplication to supplicate with for everything that distressed him, and Ali used to teach it to his son: "O Existing One before everything" up to his saying: "do with me such and such."

(15) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Ad-Dahhak who said: The supplication of Musa, peace be upon him, when he headed towards Pharaoh, and the supplication of the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, on the day of Hunayn, and the supplication of every distressed person is this: "You were and You will be" up to his saying: "O Ever-Living (Ya Hayy), O Sustainer of all existence (Ya Qayyum)."

(16) The two Sheikhs (Al-Bukhari and Muslim) recorded in their Sahihs from Ibn Abbas, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, that the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, used to say during distress: "There is no deity but God (Allah), The Supreme (al-Azim), The Forbearing (al-Halim)" up to his saying: "The Lord of the Noble Throne (Rabb al-Arsh al-Karim)." Imam An-Nawawi said in the explanation of Muslim in the chapter of Remembrance during distress and grave matters: At-Tabari said: The predecessors used to supplicate with it and call it the supplication of distress. If it is said this is praise and there is no supplication in it, he answered it from two angles: One of them is that this remembrance is used to open the supplication, then he supplicates with what he wills. And the second is the answer of Sufyan bin Uyaynah, and the poet said:
*If a man praises you one day, the praise suffices him from having to explicitly present his request.*
End of the speech of Imam An-Nawawi.

(17) In *Hall al-Iqal*, this supplication is mentioned from Ibn Abbas, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "O Ever-Living (Ya Hayy), O Forbearing (Ya Halim)" up to his saying "There is no deity but You." He mentioned it like this with truncated wording, and perhaps its deficiency in *Hall al-Iqal* is from the copyist.

(18) Imam Al-Ghazali said in *Al-Ihya*: If distress afflicts you, say: "There is no deity but God (Allah), The Most High (al-Ali), The Forbearing (al-Halim), there is no deity but God (Allah), The Lord of the Great Throne (Rabb al-Arsh al-Azim), there is no deity but God (Allah), The Lord of the seven heavens and The Lord of the Noble Throne (Rabb as-Samawati as-Sab' wa Rabb al-Arsh al-Karim)." He said he extracted it from the hadith of Ibn Abbas, and Az-Zubaydi mentioned some of the wordings: so whoever wishes to view that, he may, and its mention will come in the upcoming Fortresses with slight variations in some wordings from other narrations.

(19) When the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, went out to Ta'if on his feet and called them to Islam but they did not answer him, he departed and came to the shade of a tree, prayed two units of prayer, then said: "O God (Allahumma), to You I complain" up to his saying: "and there is no power nor strength except by You." Ibn Ad-Durayhim Al-Mawsili mentioned it in his book *Ghayat Al-Maghnam*, and the authors of the Sira have also mentioned it.

(20) From Abu Umamah, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "Indeed, God (Allah) has an angel entrusted with whoever says: O Most Merciful of the merciful (Ya Arham ar-Rahimin). So whoever says it three times, the angel says to him: The Most Merciful of the merciful (Arham ar-Rahimin) has turned His attention to you, so ask for your need." And Al-Hakim recorded from Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, passed by a man while he was saying: "O Most Merciful of the merciful (Ya Arham ar-Rahimin)." So he said to him: "Ask, for God (Allah) has looked at you."

(21) From Uthman bin Hunaif, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that he was with the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, when a blind man came to him and complained to him about the loss of his sight and said: "O Messenger of God (Allah), I have no guide and it has become difficult for me." So the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said to him: "Go to the ablution place, perform ablution, then pray two units of prayer, then say: O God (Allahumma), I ask You" up to his saying: "O God (Allahumma), accept his intercession for me." Uthman said: By God (Allah), we did not part, nor did our conversation prolong, until the man entered and it was as if there had never been any affliction in him. Abu Dawud, At-Tirmidhi, and others narrated it, and Al-Hakim authenticated it in his *Mustadrak*.

(22) Ibn Abi Hatim narrated in the book of Supplication from Musa bin Uqbah that Jibril, peace be upon him, said to the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him: "I have not been sent to anyone more beloved to me than you. Shall I not teach you a supplication I have chosen for you that I have not taught to anyone before you, to supplicate with in desire and fear: O Light of the heavens and the earth (Ya Nur as-samawati wal-ard)" up to his saying: "For indeed You see my condition and the condition of my companions." He said: So God (Allah) struck down his enemy, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him.

**(From the supplications of relief of the Prophets, peace and blessings be upon them)**

(23) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Yahya bin Sulaym that it reached him that the Angel of Death asked his Lord (Rabb) for permission to greet Yaqub (Jacob), peace be upon him, so He permitted him. He came to him and greeted him. He (Yaqub) said to him: "By the One who created you, have you taken the soul of Yusuf (Joseph)?" He said: "No." He said to him: "Shall I not teach you words by which you do not ask God (Allah) for anything except He gives it to you?" He said: "Yes." He said: "So say: O Possessor of continuous favor which never ceases and which none but Him can count (Ya Dha al-Ma'ruf alladhi la yanqati'u abadan wa la yuhsihi ghayruh)." He said: So dawn did not break until he was brought the shirt of Yusuf.

(24) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Ibrahim bin Khallad who said: Jibril descended upon Yaqub, peace be upon them both, so he complained to him about what he was in. He said: "Shall I not teach you a supplication that if you supplicate with it, God (Allah) will bring relief to you?" He said: "Yes." He said: "Say: O He whom no one knows how He is except Him" up to his saying: "bring relief to me." So the bearer of good news came to him.

(25) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Muhammad bin Umar from a man of the people of Kufa that Jibril entered upon Yusuf, peace be upon him, in the prison and said: "Say: O God (Allahumma), O Witness who is not absent (Ya Shahidan ghayra gha'ib)" up to his saying: "and provide for me from where I do not expect." As-Suyuti said it. And Ibn Ad-Durayhim mentioned in *Ghayat Al-Maghnam* from Al-Khattab bin Uthman with his chain. And Al-Khara'iti recorded in *Makarim Al-Akhlaq* from Abdullah bin Alqamah At-Ta'i that Jibril came to Yusuf, peace be upon them both, in the prison and said: "I have been informed to teach you words; perhaps God (Allah) will benefit you by them. Say: O God (Allahumma), make for me from every distress that distresses me: a relief and a way out, and provide for me from where I do not expect."

(26) Al-Khatib recorded with a chain containing unknown narrators from Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, as elevated (to the Prophet) that when God (Allah) wanted to raise Isa (Jesus) to heaven, God (Allah) dictated to him a supplication, so Isa, peace be upon him, supplicated with it, which is: "Say: O God (Allahumma), I ask You by Your Name, The One (al-Wahid), The Unique (al-Ahad)" up to his saying: "and what I have entered the morning in." So Isa, peace be upon him, supplicated with it, and God (Allah), The Mighty and Majestic (Azza wa Jall), revealed to Jibril, peace be upon him, to: "Raise My servant to Me."

(27) This supplication, "O God (Allahumma), just as You were gentle..." to its end, was mentioned by Imam Al-Ghazali in *Al-Ihya* in the book of Enjoining Good, and he mentioned a story for it, the summary of which is that Abu Ja'far Al-Mansur, while he was circumambulating at night, heard a man saying: "O God (Allahumma), I complain to You of the appearance of transgression and corruption" in a speech. So he ordered him to be brought. The man was brought to him and confronted the man (Al-Mansur) by mentioning his injustices and admonished him severely. Al-Mansur wept, then later asked about the man but could not find him. They searched for him and one of his inner circle found him. He said: "I am not going with you." He replied: "If you do not go with me, he will kill me." He said to him: "He is not capable of that," and took out a parchment for him containing this supplication. He said: "Take it and put it in your pocket, for in it is the supplication of relief." He said: "And what is the supplication of relief?" He said: "None is granted it except the martyrs. Whoever supplicates with it evening and morning, his sins are demolished, his joy lasts, his mistakes are erased, his supplication is answered, his provision is expanded, his hope is given, he is aided against his enemy, he is written with God (Allah) as a truthful one, and he does not die except as a martyr. You say: O God (Allahumma), just as You were gentle in Your greatness beyond the gentle ones" to the end of the supplication. He said: So I took it and put it in my pocket, then I had no concern other than the Commander of the Faithful. I entered and greeted him. He raised his head, looked at me, smiled, then said: "Woe to you, do you practice magic?" I said: "No, by God (Allah)." Then I told him my story with the old man. He said: "Bring the parchment he gave you," and he ordered it to be copied, and gave me ten thousand dirhams. Then he said: "Do you know him?" I said: "No." He said: "That was Al-Khidr, peace be upon him." End of the summary from *Al-Ihya*. And he mentioned the story in *Al-Manhaj Al-Hanif* and the supplication, and added at its end: "For indeed You said, and Your saying is the truth: God (Allah) is Subtle (Latif) with His servants, He provides for whom He wills, and He is The Strong (al-Qawi), The Exalted in Might (al-Aziz). O Subtle/Gentle (Ya Latif), O All-Aware (Ya Khabir), O Preserver (Ya Hafiz)." Az-Zubaydi said in the explanation of *Al-Ihya*: "And there is no harm to add after his saying 'that': and may the peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon our master Muhammad, his family, and his companions."

(28) Az-Zubaydi mentioned it in the explanation of *Al-Ihya* in the book of Enjoining Good from Al-Khidr, peace be upon him. He said: And in the book of Supplication by At-Tabarani from the route of Muhammad bin Al-Muhajir, he said: Yahya bin Muhammad Al-Hammar narrated to us, Al-Mu'alla bin Harami narrated to us, from Muhammad bin Al-Muhajir Al-Basri, Abu Abdullah Ibn At-Taw'am Ar-Raqqashi narrated to me that Sulayman bin Abd Al-Malik frightened a man and sought him to kill him. The man fled, and his messengers kept frequenting the house of that man searching for him but could not capture him. The man would not come to a town except it was said to him: "You were being sought here." When the matter prolonged for him, he resolved to go to a town where Sulayman had no rule. (Then he mentioned a long story), then he said: "While he was in a desert with no trees or water, suddenly there was a man praying." He said: "So I feared him, then I returned to myself and said: By God (Allah), it is neither a riding camel nor a beast." He said: "So I headed towards it, and he bowed and prostrated, then turned to me and said: 'Perhaps this tyrant has frightened you?' I said: 'Yes.' He said: 'So what prevented you from the Seven?' I said: 'May God (Allah) have mercy on you, and what are the Seven?' He said: 'Say: Glory be to The One (al-Wahid) other than whom there is no deity' up to his saying: 'and You are the One who is over all things without being taught.'" Then he said: "He had not finished his speech until safety was cast into my heart. And I returned back from my path wanting my family. I said: I will surely come to the door of Sulayman bin Abd Al-Malik. So I came to his door, and behold, it was his day of permission, and he was giving permission to the people. I entered, and he was upon his bed. As soon as he saw me, he sat upright on his bed, then gestured to me, and kept bringing me closer until I sat with him on the bed. Then he said: 'Did you bewitch me, or are you also a magician despite what reached me about you?' I said: 'O Commander of the Faithful, I am not a magician, nor do I know magic, nor did I bewitch you.' He said: 'Then how? For I did not think my rule would be complete except by your death. Yet when I saw you, I could not settle until I called you and sat you with me on my bed.' Then he said: 'Tell me the truth of your matter.' So I informed him. Abu Al-Abbas Al-Khidr said: 'By God (Allah), besides whom there is no deity, the one who taught them to you... Write for him a security, improve his reward, and carry him to his family.'"

(29) Al-Abiwardi narrated with his chain from Ali bin Abi Talib, may God (Allah) be pleased with him and honor his face, who said: Nebuchadnezzar was brought Daniel the Prophet, peace be upon him. He ordered concerning him, so he was imprisoned, and he provoked two lions and threw them into a pit with him, and sealed it with clay over him and the two lions. He remained in the pit with them for five days. Then he opened the pit and found Daniel standing in prayer, and the two lions were in a corner of the pit not having harmed him. Nebuchadnezzar said: "What did you say that defended you?" He said: "I said: Praise be to God (Allah) who does not forget the one who remembers Him" up to his saying: "who rewards with patience salvation." Al-Mawsili mentioned it.

**(Referencing what is mentioned in the First Fortress of the supplications of relief from the Companions and others, may God (Allah) be pleased with them)**

(30) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from a man whom Al-Hajjaj seized, shackled him in a house, and locked him in. He said: "I heard a caller in the corner: 'O so-and-so, supplicate with this supplication: O He whom no one knows how He is except Him' up to his saying: 'bring relief to me from what I am in.'" He said: "By God (Allah), I did not finish it until the shackles fell from my feet, and I looked at the doors opened, so I passed through."

(31) Al-Mundhiri recorded in his History from Muhammad bin Abd Al-Warith bin Jarir who said: We were with Al-Harith bin Miskin when Ali bin Abi Al-Qasim bin Muhriz Al-Kufi, the reciter, came to him and said: "I saw Umar bin Al-Khattab, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, in a dream and he said: 'Go to Al-Harith and convey my greetings to him, and tell him: Judge between the people, by the sign that you were in the prison in Iraq, you got up at night and stubbed your toe, so you supplicated with that supplication and were released the next day.'" Al-Harith said to him: "You have spoken the truth, and this is something no one knew about except God (Allah)." So he said to him: "And what is the supplication?" He said: "I said: O my Companion at every hardship" up to his saying: "a relief and a way out."

(32) Ad-Dinawari recorded in *Al-Mujalasah* from Abd Al-Jabbar bin Kulayb who said: We were with Ibrahim bin Adham on a journey when a lion blocked us. Ibrahim said: "Say: O God (Allahumma), guard us" up to his saying: "O Most Generous of the generous (Ya Akram al-Akramin)." He said: "So the lion turned away from us." He said: "And I supplicate with it at every terrifying thing, and I have not seen except good."

(33) Abu Bakr Muhammad bin Al-Walid At-Turtushi mentioned in the book of Supplication from Mutarrif bin Abdullah bin Mus'ab Al-Madani who said: I entered upon Al-Mansur and saw him grieved. He said to me: "O Mutarrif, a distress has struck me that none can remove except God (Allah), so is there a supplication I can supplicate with, perhaps God (Allah) will remove it from me?" I said: "O Commander of the Faithful, Muhammad bin Thabit narrated to me from Amr bin Thabit Al-Basri who said: A mosquito entered into the ear of a man from the people of Basra until it went into his ear canal; it swelled and kept him awake. A man from the companions of Al-Hasan Al-Basri said to him: 'Supplicate with the supplication of Al-Ala' bin Al-Hadrami, the companion of the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, which he supplicated with in the desert and in the sea, and God (Allah), The Glorified (Subhanahu), saved him.' He said: 'And what is it?' He said: 'Al-Ala' bin Al-Hadrami was sent to Bahrain (name of a place). They traversed a desert and suffered severe thirst until they feared perishing. He dismounted, prayed two units of prayer, then said: O All-Wise (Ya Hakim), O All-Knowing (Ya Alim), O Most High (Ya Ali), O Supreme (Ya Azim), give us drink. A cloud came and rained until they filled the vessels and watered the mounts. Then they proceeded to a gulf of the sea that had not been waded into before that day, and they did not find ships. He prayed two units of prayer, then said: O All-Wise (Ya Hakim), O All-Knowing (Ya Alim), O Most High (Ya Ali), O Supreme (Ya Azim), let us pass. Then he took the reins of his horse, then said: Pass in the name of God (Allah). Abu Hurairah said: So we walked on the water, and by God (Allah), not a foot, nor a hoof, nor a pad got wet, and the army was four thousand.' So the man supplicated with it, and he had not returned until it came out of his ear buzzing until it struck the wall, and he was cured. So Al-Mansur faced the Qibla and supplicated with this supplication for an hour, then turned his face to me and said: 'O Mutarrif, God (Allah) has removed from me the distress I was feeling.'"

(34) In the Sahih and others, a Bedouin woman used to serve the wives of the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, and she used to frequently say:
*And the day of the sash is from the wonders of our Lord (Rabb)*
*For He truly saved me from the darkness of disbelief.*
So Aisha, may God (Allah) be pleased with her, asked her about that, and she said: "I attended a bride of ours being presented. I entered with her and she had a sash on. She took it off, and a kite (bird of prey) came and took it. They missed it, accused me of it, and searched me, even my private parts. So I supplicated to God (Allah) to declare my innocence, and the kite came with the sash until it threw it among them." And in a narration: "So I raised my head and said: O Savior of those who seek help (Ya Ghiyath al-Mustaghithin)." Al-Halabi said it. And in *Siraj Al-Muluk* by Abu Bakr At-Turtushi, the king of Sicily suffered insomnia one night and sleep was prevented. So he sent a boat to Africa to check on its affairs. They encountered a drowning man in the sea saying: "O Savior of those who seek help (Ya Ghiyath al-Mustaghithin)." So they rescued him while he was in his last breaths by the blessing of this supplication.

(35) Al-Bayhaqi narrated in *Fada'il Al-A'mal* from Hammad bin Salamah bin Asim with the chain of Abu Ghunaym, the reciter of his time, who said: Poverty struck me, so I went to one of my brothers and informed him. I saw dislike in his face, so I left his house to the graveyard. I prayed whatever God (Allah) willed, then I placed my face on the earth and said: "O Causer of causes (Ya Musabbib al-Asbab)" up to his saying: "and enrich me by Your bounty from whoever is besides You." He said: "I did not raise my head until I heard a thud near me. I raised my head, and there was a kite that had dropped a red pouch. I took the pouch, and in it were eighty dinars and a jewel wrapped in cotton. I sold the jewel for a massive sum, and the dinars remained. I bought real estate from it and praised God (Allah) for that."

(36) Abu Nu'aym narrated in *Al-Hilyah* from Yahya bin Abd Al-Hamid Al-Himmani who said: I was in the gathering of Sufyan bin Uyaynah when suddenly a man fainted. He woke up and cried out: "A thousand dinars or so!" He was asked, "What is the news?" The man said: "I was on a journey." They propped him up, and he sat and said: "My father narrated to me from my grandfather that a man known as Ibn Humayr had piety; he fasted the day and stood the night in prayer. God (Allah) is a witness against me for that, and His angels, His prophets, the bearers of His throne, and the inhabitants of His heavens, if I kill you.' Muhammad said: So I opened my mouth and she slithered into it. Then I proceeded, and a man met me carrying a *Samsamah*, meaning a broad sword. He said: 'O Muhammad.' I said: 'What do you want?' She said: 'O Muhammad, did you forget the enmity that was between me and your father Adam when I got him expelled from Paradise? I said to her: 'Then give me a respite until I walk to the bottom of this mountain so I can prepare a place for myself.' She said: 'As you wish.' Muhammad said: So I proceeded heading for the mountain, having despaired of life. Then I walked, and a man smelling pleasant, pure of dirt, met me and said to me: 'Peace be upon you.' I said: 'And upon you be peace, O my brother.' He said: 'Why do I see your color has changed?' I said: 'From an enemy who has wronged me.' He said: 'And where is your enemy?' I said: 'In my stomach.' He said to me: 'Open your mouth.' So I opened my mouth, and he placed in it something like a green olive leaf. Then he said: 'Chew and swallow.' So I chewed and swallowed. He laughed, then said: 'Do you not recognize me?' I said: 'O God (Allahumma), no.' O Muhammad, you must practice doing favors (*al-ma'ruf*), for it protects from evil endings, and even if it is wronged by the one it was done for, it is not lost with God (Allah), The Mighty and Majestic (Azza wa Jall).'"

Sheikh Abu Al-Abbas Ahmad bin Muhammad bin Hasan Al-Lawati informed us, saying: Abu Al-Husain Yahya bin Muhammad, known as Ibn As-Sa'igh, informed us, saying: Abu Al-Qasim bin Khalaf bin Abd Al-Malik bin Mas'ud bin Bashkuwal informed us, saying: Abu Al-Hasan Muhammad bin Abd Ar-Rahman our companion informed us by my reading to him, Abu Al-Qasim bin Sawab informed us by hearing, Abu Marwan Abd Al-Malik bin Ziyadat Allah At-Tubni informed us, Abu Al-Qasim bin Bundar narrated to us, Muhammad bin Ali bin Al-Harawi Ash-Shafi'i narrated to me, Ahmad bin Mansur bin Muhammad Al-Hafiz informed us, Abu Al-Hasan Ali bin Al-Husain bin Hayy Al-Qattan Al-Muhtasib Al-Balkhi informed us, and he was truthful, Muhammad bin Harun Al-Hashimi informed us, Muhammad bin Yahya Al-Mazini narrated to us, Musa bin Sahl narrated to us from Ar-Rabi' who said: When the caliphate passed to Abu Ja'far Al-Mansur, he said to me: "O Rabi', send for Ja'far bin Muhammad." Then I came to him after an hour, and he said: "Did I not tell you to send for Ja'far bin Muhammad? By God (Allah), you will bring him to me or I will kill you a most evil killing." Ja'far said: "My father narrated to me from his father from his grandfather that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: 'A banner will be set up for the treacherous on the Day of Resurrection by which he will be known.'" Ja'far said: "My father narrated to me from his father from his grandfather that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: 'A caller will call on the Day of Resurrection in the depths of the Throne: Let him stand whose reward is upon God (Allah). So none from the servants of God (Allah) will stand except the pardoning ones.'" He did not cease speaking until what was with him (Al-Mansur's anger) subsided. Then he said: "Sit, O Abu Abdullah." Then he said to me: "O Rabi', follow him with his prize and double it for him." He said: So I followed him and said: "O Abu Abdullah, you know my love for you." He said: "You are from us. My father narrated to me from his father from his grandfather that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: 'The freed slave of a people is from them.'" I said: "O Abu Abdullah, I witnessed what you did not witness, and I saw what you did not see, and death hovered over you repeatedly. With what were you moving your lips when you entered? He said: "Rather, something my father narrated to me from his father from his grandfather, that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, whenever a matter pressed hard on him, supplicated with this supplication, and he used to say it is the supplication of relief, and it is this: O God (Allahumma), guard me with Your eye that does not sleep" up to his saying: "and there is no power nor strength except by God (Allah), The Most High (al-Ali), The Supreme (al-Azim)." Ar-Rabi' said: So I wrote it down from Ja'far bin Muhammad, and here it is in my pocket. Musa bin Sahl said: So I wrote it down from Ar-Rabi', and here it is in my pocket. (72) At-Tabarani narrated from Ibn Abbas, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "Whoever says: There is no deity but God (Allah) before everything, there is no deity but God (Allah) after everything, and there is no deity but God (Allah), our Lord (Rabb) remains and everything perishes; will be saved from worry."

(73) Al-Khara'iti narrated in *Makarim Al-Akhlaq* from Ali, may God (Allah) be pleased with him and honor his face, that the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, taught him words to say at the ruler and at everything he fears: "There is no deity but God (Allah), The Forbearing (al-Halim), The Generous (al-Karim)" up to his saying: "I seek refuge in You from the evil of Your servants."

(74) Al-Hakim narrated in his History from Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "Whoever says every day four times: I bear witness that God (Allah), He is The Clear Truth (al-Haqq al-Mubin)" up to his saying: "He resurrects those in the graves; God (Allah) will turn evil away from him."

(75) Ibn Jarir narrated from Ibn Abbas, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, that the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, held the two side posts of a door while we were in the house and said: "O Banu Abd Al-Muttalib, is there anyone among you from other than you?" They said: "A son of our sister." He said: "The son of the sister of a people is from them." Then he said: "O Banu Abd Al-Muttalib, if distress, hardship, or adversity befalls you, then say: God (Allah), God (Allah), He has no partner."

(76) At-Tabarani narrated in Al-Awsat from Aisha, may God (Allah) be pleased with her, from the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, that he said: "If worry or adversity afflicts one of you, let him say: God (Allah), God (Allah) is my Lord (Rabb), I do not associate anything with Him." And An-Nasa'i narrated it from Umar bin Abd Al-Aziz with the wording: "If worry or sorrow afflicts one of you, let him say seven times..." and he mentioned it.
