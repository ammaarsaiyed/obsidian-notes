**Referencing of the Prophetic Remembrances and Supplications**

(1) From Ali, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, he said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said to me: "O Ali, shall I not teach you words that if you fall into a predicament you say them?" I said: "Yes, may God (Allah) make me your ransom." He said: "If you fall into a predicament, say: In the name of God (Allah), The Most Compassionate (ar-Rahman), The Most Merciful (ar-Rahim), and there is no power nor strength except by God (Allah), The Most High (al-Ali), The Supreme (al-Azim). For indeed, God (Allah), The Exalted (Ta'ala), turns away by them whatever He wills from the types of affliction."

(2) Al-Hakim recorded from Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: We were with the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, and a man was standing praying. When he bowed, prostrated, recited the Tashahhud, and supplicated, he said in his supplication: "O God (Allahumma), I ask You by the fact that to You belongs the praise" up to his saying: "O Ever-Living (Ya Hayy), O Sustainer of all existence (Ya Qayyum)." The Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "He has supplicated to God (Allah) by His Greatest Name, which if He is called by it He answers, and if He is asked by it He gives."

(3) An-Nasa'i recorded from Ibn Mas'ud, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, used to supplicate: "O God (Allahumma), I ask You by Your Name which if He is called by it He answers, and if He is asked by it He gives."

(4) Al-Bukhari recorded in Al-Adab Al-Mufrad from Ibn Abbas, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, who said: "Whoever is befallen by distress, or sorrow, or anguish, or fears a ruler, and supplicates with these words, he will be answered: O God (Allahumma), I ask You by there is no deity but You" up to his saying: "Indeed, You are over all things competent (Qadir)." And it is apparent that Ibn Abbas narrates it from the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him.

(5) Al-Bazzar and Abu Ash-Shaikh recorded in *Ath-Thawab* from Aisha, may God (Allah) be pleased with her, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "If the servant says 'O Lord (Ya Rabb), O Lord (Ya Rabb)' four times, God (Allah) says: 'At your service, My servant, ask and you shall be given.'" And from Jabir similar to it, narrated by Ad-Daylami.

(6) Al-Bukhari recorded in Al-Adab Al-Mufrad from Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: I was with the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, and a man supplicated and said: "O Originator of the heavens (Ya Badi' as-Samawat), O Ever-Living (Ya Hayy), O Sustainer of all existence (Ya Qayyum), I ask You." The Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "Do you know by what he supplicated? By the One in whose hand is my soul, he has supplicated to God (Allah) by His Name which if He is called by it, He answers."

(7) At-Tabarani recorded in Al-Awsat from Anas that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, entered upon Aisha, may God (Allah) be pleased with her, one morning and she said: "O Messenger of God (Allah), teach me the Greatest Name of God (Allah) which if He is called by it He answers, and if He is asked by it He gives." So he turned his face away. She stood, performed ablution, and said: "O God (Allahumma), I ask You for all good" up to her saying: "and if You are asked by it, You give." He said: "By God (Allah), it is indeed within these names."

(8) At-Tabarani recorded in Al-Kabir and Al-Awsat with a good chain from Mu'awiyah bin Abi Sufyan, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, who said: I heard the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, saying: "Whoever supplicates with these five words will not ask God (Allah) for anything except that He will give it to him: There is no deity but God (Allah) and God (Allah) is the greatest" up to his saying: "and there is no power nor strength except by God (Allah)."

(9) Imam Ahmad, Ibn Hibban, Al-Hakim, and Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Ibn Mas'ud, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "No servant is afflicted by distress or sorrow and says: 'O God (Allahumma), I am Your servant' up to his saying: 'and a departure for my distress' except that God (Allah) takes away his distress and replaces his sorrow with relief." They said: "O Messenger of God (Allah), should we not learn these words?" He said: "Yes, it behoves whoever hears them to learn them."

(10) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from the route of Al-Khalil from the jurist of the people of Jordan who said: It reached us that the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, whenever he was afflicted by sorrow or anguish, used to say: "Sufficient for me is The Lord (ar-Rabb) from the servants" up to his saying: "and He is The Lord of the Great Throne (Rabb al-Arsh al-Azim)."

(11) Ibn As-Sunni and Al-Hakim recorded from Abu Hurairah, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "No matter distressed me except that Jibril appeared to me and said: O Muhammad, say: I rely upon The Ever-Living (al-Hayy) who does not die, to the end of the verse." And Al-Hakim and At-Tabarani narrated it from Abu Hurairah.

(12) From Shaddad bin Aws, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "God is sufficient for me, and He is the best disposer of affairs (Hasbi Allah wa Ni'm al-Wakil) is a safety for every fearful person." And Imam Ja'far bin Muhammad, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, said: I marvel at one who fears harm, how does it escape him to say: 'God is sufficient for us, and He is the best disposer of affairs (Hasbuna Allah wa Ni'm al-Wakil),' while God (Allah), The Exalted (Ta'ala), says: *{So they returned with grace from God (Allah) and bounty, no harm having touched them}*. And I marvel at one who is afflicted with sorrow, how does it escape him to say: 'There is no deity but You, glory be to You, indeed I have been of the wrongdoers,' while God (Allah), The Glorified (Subhanahu), says: *{So We answered him and saved him from the distress, and thus do We save the believers}*. Al-Halabi said it.

(13) Ibn Mardawayh recorded from Abu Hurairah, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that he said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "If you fall into a great matter, then say: God is sufficient for us, and He is the best disposer of affairs (Hasbuna Allah wa Ni'm al-Wakil)."

(14) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Al-Baqir Muhammad bin Ali that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, taught Ali, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, a supplication to supplicate with for everything that distressed him, and Ali used to teach it to his son: "O Existing One before everything" up to his saying: "do with me such and such."

(15) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Ad-Dahhak who said: The supplication of Musa, peace be upon him, when he headed towards Pharaoh, and the supplication of the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, on the day of Hunayn, and the supplication of every distressed person is this: "You were and You will be" up to his saying: "O Ever-Living (Ya Hayy), O Sustainer of all existence (Ya Qayyum)."

(16) The two Sheikhs (Al-Bukhari and Muslim) recorded in their Sahihs from Ibn Abbas, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, that the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, used to say during distress: "There is no deity but God (Allah), The Supreme (al-Azim), The Forbearing (al-Halim)" up to his saying: "The Lord of the Noble Throne (Rabb al-Arsh al-Karim)." Imam An-Nawawi said in the explanation of Muslim in the chapter of Remembrance during distress and grave matters: At-Tabari said: The predecessors used to supplicate with it and call it the supplication of distress. If it is said this is praise and there is no supplication in it, he answered it from two angles: One of them is that this remembrance is used to open the supplication, then he supplicates with what he wills. And the second is the answer of Sufyan bin Uyaynah, and the poet said:
*If a man praises you one day, the praise suffices him from having to explicitly present his request.*
End of the speech of Imam An-Nawawi.

(17) In *Hall al-Iqal*, this supplication is mentioned from Ibn Abbas, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "O Ever-Living (Ya Hayy), O Forbearing (Ya Halim)" up to his saying "There is no deity but You." He mentioned it like this with truncated wording, and perhaps its deficiency in *Hall al-Iqal* is from the copyist.

(18) Imam Al-Ghazali said in *Al-Ihya*: If distress afflicts you, say: "There is no deity but God (Allah), The Most High (al-Ali), The Forbearing (al-Halim), there is no deity but God (Allah), The Lord of the Great Throne (Rabb al-Arsh al-Azim), there is no deity but God (Allah), The Lord of the seven heavens and The Lord of the Noble Throne (Rabb as-Samawati as-Sab' wa Rabb al-Arsh al-Karim)." He said he extracted it from the hadith of Ibn Abbas, and Az-Zubaydi mentioned some of the wordings: so whoever wishes to view that, he may, and its mention will come in the upcoming Fortresses with slight variations in some wordings from other narrations.

(19) When the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, went out to Ta'if on his feet and called them to Islam but they did not answer him, he departed and came to the shade of a tree, prayed two units of prayer, then said: "O God (Allahumma), to You I complain" up to his saying: "and there is no power nor strength except by You." Ibn Ad-Durayhim Al-Mawsili mentioned it in his book *Ghayat Al-Maghnam*, and the authors of the Sira have also mentioned it.

(20) From Abu Umamah, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: "Indeed, God (Allah) has an angel entrusted with whoever says: O Most Merciful of the merciful (Ya Arham ar-Rahimin). So whoever says it three times, the angel says to him: The Most Merciful of the merciful (Arham ar-Rahimin) has turned His attention to you, so ask for your need." And Al-Hakim recorded from Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, passed by a man while he was saying: "O Most Merciful of the merciful (Ya Arham ar-Rahimin)." So he said to him: "Ask, for God (Allah) has looked at you."

(21) From Uthman bin Hunaif, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that he was with the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, when a blind man came to him and complained to him about the loss of his sight and said: "O Messenger of God (Allah), I have no guide and it has become difficult for me." So the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said to him: "Go to the ablution place, perform ablution, then pray two units of prayer, then say: O God (Allahumma), I ask You" up to his saying: "O God (Allahumma), accept his intercession for me." Uthman said: By God (Allah), we did not part, nor did our conversation prolong, until the man entered and it was as if there had never been any affliction in him. Abu Dawud, At-Tirmidhi, and others narrated it, and Al-Hakim authenticated it in his *Mustadrak*.

(22) Ibn Abi Hatim narrated in the book of Supplication from Musa bin Uqbah that Jibril, peace be upon him, said to the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him: "I have not been sent to anyone more beloved to me than you. Shall I not teach you a supplication I have chosen for you that I have not taught to anyone before you, to supplicate with in desire and fear: O Light of the heavens and the earth (Ya Nur as-samawati wal-ard)" up to his saying: "For indeed You see my condition and the condition of my companions." He said: So God (Allah) struck down his enemy, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him.

**(From the supplications of relief of the Prophets, peace and blessings be upon them)**

(23) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Yahya bin Sulaym that it reached him that the Angel of Death asked his Lord (Rabb) for permission to greet Yaqub (Jacob), peace be upon him, so He permitted him. He came to him and greeted him. He (Yaqub) said to him: "By the One who created you, have you taken the soul of Yusuf (Joseph)?" He said: "No." He said to him: "Shall I not teach you words by which you do not ask God (Allah) for anything except He gives it to you?" He said: "Yes." He said: "So say: O Possessor of continuous favor which never ceases and which none but Him can count (Ya Dha al-Ma'ruf alladhi la yanqati'u abadan wa la yuhsihi ghayruh)." He said: So dawn did not break until he was brought the shirt of Yusuf.

(24) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Ibrahim bin Khallad who said: Jibril descended upon Yaqub, peace be upon them both, so he complained to him about what he was in. He said: "Shall I not teach you a supplication that if you supplicate with it, God (Allah) will bring relief to you?" He said: "Yes." He said: "Say: O He whom no one knows how He is except Him" up to his saying: "bring relief to me." So the bearer of good news came to him.

(25) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from Muhammad bin Umar from a man of the people of Kufa that Jibril entered upon Yusuf, peace be upon him, in the prison and said: "Say: O God (Allahumma), O Witness who is not absent (Ya Shahidan ghayra gha'ib)" up to his saying: "and provide for me from where I do not expect." As-Suyuti said it. And Ibn Ad-Durayhim mentioned in *Ghayat Al-Maghnam* from Al-Khattab bin Uthman with his chain. And Al-Khara'iti recorded in *Makarim Al-Akhlaq* from Abdullah bin Alqamah At-Ta'i that Jibril came to Yusuf, peace be upon them both, in the prison and said: "I have been informed to teach you words; perhaps God (Allah) will benefit you by them. Say: O God (Allahumma), make for me from every distress that distresses me: a relief and a way out, and provide for me from where I do not expect."

(26) Al-Khatib recorded with a chain containing unknown narrators from Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, as elevated (to the Prophet) that when God (Allah) wanted to raise Isa (Jesus) to heaven, God (Allah) dictated to him a supplication, so Isa, peace be upon him, supplicated with it, which is: "Say: O God (Allahumma), I ask You by Your Name, The One (al-Wahid), The Unique (al-Ahad)" up to his saying: "and what I have entered the morning in." So Isa, peace be upon him, supplicated with it, and God (Allah), The Mighty and Majestic (Azza wa Jall), revealed to Jibril, peace be upon him, to: "Raise My servant to Me."

(27) This supplication, "O God (Allahumma), just as You were gentle..." to its end, was mentioned by Imam Al-Ghazali in *Al-Ihya* in the book of Enjoining Good, and he mentioned a story for it, the summary of which is that Abu Ja'far Al-Mansur, while he was circumambulating at night, heard a man saying: "O God (Allahumma), I complain to You of the appearance of transgression and corruption" in a speech. So he ordered him to be brought. The man was brought to him and confronted the man (Al-Mansur) by mentioning his injustices and admonished him severely. Al-Mansur wept, then later asked about the man but could not find him. They searched for him and one of his inner circle found him. He said: "I am not going with you." He replied: "If you do not go with me, he will kill me." He said to him: "He is not capable of that," and took out a parchment for him containing this supplication. He said: "Take it and put it in your pocket, for in it is the supplication of relief." He said: "And what is the supplication of relief?" He said: "None is granted it except the martyrs. Whoever supplicates with it evening and morning, his sins are demolished, his joy lasts, his mistakes are erased, his supplication is answered, his provision is expanded, his hope is given, he is aided against his enemy, he is written with God (Allah) as a truthful one, and he does not die except as a martyr. You say: O God (Allahumma), just as You were gentle in Your greatness beyond the gentle ones" to the end of the supplication. He said: So I took it and put it in my pocket, then I had no concern other than the Commander of the Faithful. I entered and greeted him. He raised his head, looked at me, smiled, then said: "Woe to you, do you practice magic?" I said: "No, by God (Allah)." Then I told him my story with the old man. He said: "Bring the parchment he gave you," and he ordered it to be copied, and gave me ten thousand dirhams. Then he said: "Do you know him?" I said: "No." He said: "That was Al-Khidr, peace be upon him." End of the summary from *Al-Ihya*. And he mentioned the story in *Al-Manhaj Al-Hanif* and the supplication, and added at its end: "For indeed You said, and Your saying is the truth: God (Allah) is Subtle (Latif) with His servants, He provides for whom He wills, and He is The Strong (al-Qawi), The Exalted in Might (al-Aziz). O Subtle/Gentle (Ya Latif), O All-Aware (Ya Khabir), O Preserver (Ya Hafiz)." Az-Zubaydi said in the explanation of *Al-Ihya*: "And there is no harm to add after his saying 'that': and may the peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon our master Muhammad, his family, and his companions."

(28) Az-Zubaydi mentioned it in the explanation of *Al-Ihya* in the book of Enjoining Good from Al-Khidr, peace be upon him. He said: And in the book of Supplication by At-Tabarani from the route of Muhammad bin Al-Muhajir, he said: Yahya bin Muhammad Al-Hammar narrated to us, Al-Mu'alla bin Harami narrated to us, from Muhammad bin Al-Muhajir Al-Basri, Abu Abdullah Ibn At-Taw'am Ar-Raqqashi narrated to me that Sulayman bin Abd Al-Malik frightened a man and sought him to kill him. The man fled, and his messengers kept frequenting the house of that man searching for him but could not capture him. The man would not come to a town except it was said to him: "You were being sought here." When the matter prolonged for him, he resolved to go to a town where Sulayman had no rule. (Then he mentioned a long story), then he said: "While he was in a desert with no trees or water, suddenly there was a man praying." He said: "So I feared him, then I returned to myself and said: By God (Allah), it is neither a riding camel nor a beast." He said: "So I headed towards it, and he bowed and prostrated, then turned to me and said: 'Perhaps this tyrant has frightened you?' I said: 'Yes.' He said: 'So what prevented you from the Seven?' I said: 'May God (Allah) have mercy on you, and what are the Seven?' He said: 'Say: Glory be to The One (al-Wahid) other than whom there is no deity' up to his saying: 'and You are the One who is over all things without being taught.'" Then he said: "He had not finished his speech until safety was cast into my heart. And I returned back from my path wanting my family. I said: I will surely come to the door of Sulayman bin Abd Al-Malik. So I came to his door, and behold, it was his day of permission, and he was giving permission to the people. I entered, and he was upon his bed. As soon as he saw me, he sat upright on his bed, then gestured to me, and kept bringing me closer until I sat with him on the bed. Then he said: 'Did you bewitch me, or are you also a magician despite what reached me about you?' I said: 'O Commander of the Faithful, I am not a magician, nor do I know magic, nor did I bewitch you.' He said: 'Then how? For I did not think my rule would be complete except by your death. Yet when I saw you, I could not settle until I called you and sat you with me on my bed.' Then he said: 'Tell me the truth of your matter.' So I informed him. Abu Al-Abbas Al-Khidr said: 'By God (Allah), besides whom there is no deity, the one who taught them to you... Write for him a security, improve his reward, and carry him to his family.'"

(29) Al-Abiwardi narrated with his chain from Ali bin Abi Talib, may God (Allah) be pleased with him and honor his face, who said: Nebuchadnezzar was brought Daniel the Prophet, peace be upon him. He ordered concerning him, so he was imprisoned, and he provoked two lions and threw them into a pit with him, and sealed it with clay over him and the two lions. He remained in the pit with them for five days. Then he opened the pit and found Daniel standing in prayer, and the two lions were in a corner of the pit not having harmed him. Nebuchadnezzar said: "What did you say that defended you?" He said: "I said: Praise be to God (Allah) who does not forget the one who remembers Him" up to his saying: "who rewards with patience salvation." Al-Mawsili mentioned it.

**(Referencing what is mentioned in the First Fortress of the supplications of relief from the Companions and others, may God (Allah) be pleased with them)**

(30) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya recorded from a man whom Al-Hajjaj seized, shackled him in a house, and locked him in. He said: "I heard a caller in the corner: 'O so-and-so, supplicate with this supplication: O He whom no one knows how He is except Him' up to his saying: 'bring relief to me from what I am in.'" He said: "By God (Allah), I did not finish it until the shackles fell from my feet, and I looked at the doors opened, so I passed through."

(31) Al-Mundhiri recorded in his History from Muhammad bin Abd Al-Warith bin Jarir who said: We were with Al-Harith bin Miskin when Ali bin Abi Al-Qasim bin Muhriz Al-Kufi, the reciter, came to him and said: "I saw Umar bin Al-Khattab, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, in a dream and he said: 'Go to Al-Harith and convey my greetings to him, and tell him: Judge between the people, by the sign that you were in the prison in Iraq, you got up at night and stubbed your toe, so you supplicated with that supplication and were released the next day.'" Al-Harith said to him: "You have spoken the truth, and this is something no one knew about except God (Allah)." So he said to him: "And what is the supplication?" He said: "I said: O my Companion at every hardship" up to his saying: "a relief and a way out."

(32) Ad-Dinawari recorded in *Al-Mujalasah* from Abd Al-Jabbar bin Kulayb who said: We were with Ibrahim bin Adham on a journey when a lion blocked us. Ibrahim said: "Say: O God (Allahumma), guard us" up to his saying: "O Most Generous of the generous (Ya Akram al-Akramin)." He said: "So the lion turned away from us." He said: "And I supplicate with it at every terrifying thing, and I have not seen except good."

(33) Abu Bakr Muhammad bin Al-Walid At-Turtushi mentioned in the book of Supplication from Mutarrif bin Abdullah bin Mus'ab Al-Madani who said: I entered upon Al-Mansur and saw him grieved. He said to me: "O Mutarrif, a distress has struck me that none can remove except God (Allah), so is there a supplication I can supplicate with, perhaps God (Allah) will remove it from me?" I said: "O Commander of the Faithful, Muhammad bin Thabit narrated to me from Amr bin Thabit Al-Basri who said: A mosquito entered into the ear of a man from the people of Basra until it went into his ear canal; it swelled and kept him awake. A man from the companions of Al-Hasan Al-Basri said to him: 'Supplicate with the supplication of Al-Ala' bin Al-Hadrami, the companion of the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, which he supplicated with in the desert and in the sea, and God (Allah), The Glorified (Subhanahu), saved him.' He said: 'And what is it?' He said: 'Al-Ala' bin Al-Hadrami was sent to Bahrain (name of a place). They traversed a desert and suffered severe thirst until they feared perishing. He dismounted, prayed two units of prayer, then said: O All-Wise (Ya Hakim), O All-Knowing (Ya Alim), O Most High (Ya Ali), O Supreme (Ya Azim), give us drink. A cloud came and rained until they filled the vessels and watered the mounts. Then they proceeded to a gulf of the sea that had not been waded into before that day, and they did not find ships. He prayed two units of prayer, then said: O All-Wise (Ya Hakim), O All-Knowing (Ya Alim), O Most High (Ya Ali), O Supreme (Ya Azim), let us pass. Then he took the reins of his horse, then said: Pass in the name of God (Allah). Abu Hurairah said: So we walked on the water, and by God (Allah), not a foot, nor a hoof, nor a pad got wet, and the army was four thousand.' So the man supplicated with it, and he had not returned until it came out of his ear buzzing until it struck the wall, and he was cured. So Al-Mansur faced the Qibla and supplicated with this supplication for an hour, then turned his face to me and said: 'O Mutarrif, God (Allah) has removed from me the distress I was feeling.'"

(34) In the Sahih and others, a Bedouin woman used to serve the wives of the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, and she used to frequently say:
*And the day of the sash is from the wonders of our Lord (Rabb)*
*For He truly saved me from the darkness of disbelief.*
So Aisha, may God (Allah) be pleased with her, asked her about that, and she said: "I attended a bride of ours being presented. I entered with her and she had a sash on. She took it off, and a kite (bird of prey) came and took it. They missed it, accused me of it, and searched me, even my private parts. So I supplicated to God (Allah) to declare my innocence, and the kite came with the sash until it threw it among them." And in a narration: "So I raised my head and said: O Savior of those who seek help (Ya Ghiyath al-Mustaghithin)." Al-Halabi said it. And in *Siraj Al-Muluk* by Abu Bakr At-Turtushi, the king of Sicily suffered insomnia one night and sleep was prevented. So he sent a boat to Africa to check on its affairs. They encountered a drowning man in the sea saying: "O Savior of those who seek help (Ya Ghiyath al-Mustaghithin)." So they rescued him while he was in his last breaths by the blessing of this supplication.

(35) Al-Bayhaqi narrated in *Fada'il Al-A'mal* from Hammad bin Salamah bin Asim with the chain of Abu Ghunaym, the reciter of his time, who said: Poverty struck me, so I went to one of my brothers and informed him. I saw dislike in his face, so I left his house to the graveyard. I prayed whatever God (Allah) willed, then I placed my face on the earth and said: "O Causer of causes (Ya Musabbib al-Asbab)" up to his saying: "and enrich me by Your bounty from whoever is besides You." He said: "I did not raise my head until I heard a thud near me. I raised my head, and there was a kite that had dropped a red pouch. I took the pouch, and in it were eighty dinars and a jewel wrapped in cotton. I sold the jewel for a massive sum, and the dinars remained. I bought real estate from it and praised God (Allah) for that."

(36) Abu Nu'aym narrated in *Al-Hilyah* from Yahya bin Abd Al-Hamid Al-Himmani who said: I was in the gathering of Sufyan bin Uyaynah when suddenly a man fainted. He woke up and cried out: "A thousand dinars or so!" He was asked, "What is the news?" The man said: "I was on a journey." They propped him up, and he sat and said: "My father narrated to me from my grandfather that a man known as Ibn Humayr had piety; he fasted the day and stood the night in prayer. God (Allah) is a witness against me for that, and His angels, His prophets, the bearers of His throne, and the inhabitants of His heavens, if I kill you.' Muhammad said: So I opened my mouth and she slithered into it. Then I proceeded, and a man met me carrying a *Samsamah*, meaning a broad sword. He said: 'O Muhammad.' I said: 'What do you want?' She said: 'O Muhammad, did you forget the enmity that was between me and your father Adam when I got him expelled from Paradise? I said to her: 'Then give me a respite until I walk to the bottom of this mountain so I can prepare a place for myself.' She said: 'As you wish.' Muhammad said: So I proceeded heading for the mountain, having despaired of life. Then I walked, and a man smelling pleasant, pure of dirt, met me and said to me: 'Peace be upon you.' I said: 'And upon you be peace, O my brother.' He said: 'Why do I see your color has changed?' I said: 'From an enemy who has wronged me.' He said: 'And where is your enemy?' I said: 'In my stomach.' He said to me: 'Open your mouth.' So I opened my mouth, and he placed in it something like a green olive leaf. Then he said: 'Chew and swallow.' So I chewed and swallowed. He laughed, then said: 'Do you not recognize me?' I said: 'O God (Allahumma), no.' O Muhammad, you must practice doing favors (*al-ma'ruf*), for it protects from evil endings, and even if it is wronged by the one it was done for, it is not lost with God (Allah), The Mighty and Majestic (Azza wa Jall).'"

The blind man woke up and started listening to his supplication, so he said: “O God (Allah), The Lord (ar-Rabb) of perishable spirits... up to his saying: as long as You keep me alive.” He said: The blind man memorized this supplication, then he stood up, performed ablution, prayed two rak‘ahs, and supplicated with it, and by morning, God (Allah) had restored his sight to him.

One is for hardships, and it is: “O Existent... up to his saying: make me sincerely devoted to You.” And the other is for the fulfillment of needs, and it is: “O He who suffices... up to his saying: and he asks his need.” Al-Mawsili mentioned it.

(103) It is narrated from Al-Hasan Al-Basri that he said: Whoever consistently recites these five verses in hardships, God (Allah) will relieve him of them, and they are His saying, Exalted is He: {And [mention] the man of the fish, when he went off in anger} up to His saying: {save the believers}. And His saying, Exalted is He: {And We will surely test you with something of fear and hunger} up to His saying: {And it is those who are the [rightly] guided}. And His saying, Exalted is He: {Those to whom hypocrites said} up to His saying: {And God (Allah) is the possessor of great bounty}. And His saying, Exalted is He: {And [mention] Job, when he called to his Lord} up to His saying: {and a reminder for the worshippers}. And His saying, Exalted is He: {And I entrust my affair to God (Allah). Indeed, God (Allah) is Seeing of [His] servants}. ‘Abdullah Al-Halabi mentioned it.

(104) Ibn ‘Asakir extracted from Sa‘id bin Al-Musayyab who said: A matter befell me that distressed me, so I went out during the night to the mosque of the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him. I entered the mosque, and I heard the movement of pebbles. I turned around but saw no one, and I heard a comer saying: “Supplicate to God (Allah) in this matter that distresses you and say: ‘O God (Allah), I ask You... up to his saying: and whatever You will of a matter, will be.’” He said: “So I never supplicated to God (Allah) with it for anything except that I saw it [fulfilled].”

(105) “O God (Allah), send prayers, peace, and blessings upon our master Muhammad and upon the family of our master Muhammad. My means have run out; catch up to me (rescue me), O Messenger of God (Allah).” This prayer is tested for relieving distress. The Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, taught it in a dream to the Mufti of the Levant, Hamid Effendi Al-‘Imadi, when a certain vizier of Damascus wanted to assault him. When he recited it, God (Allah) the Exalted relieved his distress. Ibn ‘Abidin mentioned that he and others tested it, and God (Allah) relieved them. I have also tested it myself, and God (Allah) brought relief to me, and many brethren informed me that they tested it, and God (Allah) brought them relief.

(106) The Hizb of An-Nawawi. The erudite scholar Sheikh Ahmad bin Muhammad An-Nakhli Al-Makki Ash-Shafi‘i said in his *Thabat* in which he mentioned his teachers and his chains of transmission for his narrations from them: I took the Hizb of the valiant Imam, the divine pole, the Lordly scholar, the saint (Wali) of God (Allah) the Exalted without dispute, and the editor of the Shafi‘i school of thought without defense, Imam Muhyi Ad-Din Abu Zakariya Yahya bin Sharaf An-Nawawi, may God (Allah) the Exalted benefit us and the Muslims by him. Amen. [I took it] from our Sheikh, Sheikh ‘Ali bin Al-Jamal Al-Ansari, may God (Allah) the Exalted have vast mercy on him in this world and the Hereafter, and he authorized me to read it after the dawn (Subh) prayer once and after the sunset (Maghrib) prayer once. He mentioned from his teachers, may God (Allah) the Exalted have mercy on them, that the reader of this Hizb is protected from the evil of Jinn and mankind, and from the people of the heavens and the people of the earth, and from the assaults of the saints (Awliya’), the people of hearts who have inward disposal through spiritual states, and from the machinations of the corrupt outwardly in all that they do of magic, trickery, deceit, deception, and other than that. And it is this: “In the name of God (Allah), God (Allah) is the Greatest...” to the end of it. Then he said after mentioning the whole Hizb: And our Sheikh, Sheikh ‘Ali bin Al-Jamal, may God (Allah) the Exalted have mercy on him, took the Hizb of Imam An-Nawawi, may God (Allah) the Exalted have mercy on him, from Sheikh Muhammad As-Satiha, from my master Sheikh Ahmad bin ‘Ali bin ‘Abd Al-Quddus Ash-Shinnawi, from his father ‘Ali, from my master Sheikh ‘Abd Al-Wahhab Ash-Sha‘rani, from Al-Burhan bin Abi Sharif Al-Maqdisi, from Al-Badr Al-Qabbani, from Ibn Al-Khabbaz, from its author Imam Muhyi Ad-Din bin Sharaf An-Nawawi, may God (Allah) the Exalted have vast mercy on him, and benefit us and the Muslims by him in this world and the Hereafter. Amen. An-Nakhli said: And our Sheikh, Sheikh Muhammad Al-Babili, our Sheikh, Sheikh ‘Isa Al-Maghribi, and our Sheikh, Sheikh ‘Abdullah bin Sa‘id Baqashir, authorized me to read the Hizb of Imam Muhyi Ad-Din Yahya An-Nawawi. Sheikh ‘Abdullah Baqashir ordered me to read “I have hidden myself...” up to the end of the Hizb three times. Some of the saintly scholars who know God (Allah) the Exalted said: And whoever reads the Hizb of Imam An-Nawawi, may God (Allah) the Exalted have mercy on him, in the morning ten times, he will have through it abundant opening in outward and inward actions, words, and states, and God (Allah) knows best. End of the words of Sheikh Ahmad An-Nakhli, may God (Allah) the Exalted have mercy on him.
And the famous erudite Imam, Sheikh Muhammad Al-Amir Al-Kabir Al-Misri Al-Maliki, said at the end of his *Thabat*, his chain of transmission reporting the Hizb of Imam An-Nawawi, and he did not mention any other Ahzab in it. He said: I narrate it from the teacher Al-Hifni, from Sheikh Muhammad bin ‘Ali Al-‘Alawi, from my master Muhammad bin Sa‘d Ad-Din, from my master Muhammad bin At-Turjuman, from my master ‘Abd Al-Wahhab Ash-Sha‘rani, from Al-Burhan bin Abi Sharif, from Al-Badr Al-Qabbani, from my master Muhammad Al-Khabbaz, from its author. End of the words of Al-Amir.
And my master, the erudite investigator, Sayyid Muhammad ‘Abidin Al-Hanafi Ad-Dimashqi, said in his *Thabat* named *‘Uqud Al-La’ali fi Al-Asanid Al-‘Awali*: The Hizb of the valiant Imam, the divine pole, begins with: “In the name of God (Allah), God (Allah) is the Greatest, God (Allah) is the Greatest, God (Allah) is the Greatest. I say over myself...” to the end of it. It is more famous among Arabs and non-Arabs than a fire on a banner (mountain peak). Ash-Shihab An-Nakhli mentioned it in its entirety in his *Thabat* and mentioned that he took it from his Sheikh ‘Ali bin Al-Jamal, and quoted his previous expression. Then Ibn ‘Abidin said: I say, I took it via reading and hearing in Damietta from our Sheikh, the teacher Sayyid Muhammad bin Ahmad bin ‘Abd Al-Qadir Al-Baha’i, the servant of the Prophetic Sunnah there, and he permitted me to read it and informed me that he read it to his two teachers, the erudite scholar Sheikh Muhammad Al-Budayri Ad-Dimyati and Sayyid Mansur Al-Halabi, and they ordered him to read it, and they both [took it] from the pole Muhammad Al-Hifni, and he mentioned the rest of his previous chain in the words of Al-Amir up to Imam An-Nawawi.
And my master, the knowing Imam, the great renowned saint, Sayyid Mustafa Al-Bakri Al-Hanafi, may God (Allah) the Exalted have mercy on him, said at the beginning of his commentary on the Hizb of An-Nawawi after mentioning a group of his virtues and miracles, may God (Allah) be pleased with him: And let us now mention what some prominent figures mentioned regarding the virtue of the Hizb. The commentator of *Ad-Dala’il*, who grazes in the garden of problematic issues, Muhammad Al-Mahdi bin Ahmad bin ‘Ali Al-Fasi, may God (Allah) the Exalted sanctify my breaths by himself, said regarding his saying: “Neither a righteous person nor a wicked one.” This is similar to what was transmitted from the Pole Sheikh, Jamal Ad-Din, my master Yusuf bin ‘Abdullah bin ‘Umar bin ‘Ali bin Khidr Al-Kurani Al-‘Ajami, a resident of Egypt, regarding whoever persistently reads the Hizb of An-Nawawi, may God (Allah) the Exalted have mercy on him, after the dawn and sunset prayers, or he said after the dawn and night prayers, that no one can act upon him, neither from the people of the inward, the masters of hearts who dispose by the truth—or he said by sound states—nor from the people of the outward, the people of trickery, magic, deception, war, dispute, and enmity, and God (Allah) the Exalted knows best. End quote. Sayyid Mustafa Al-Bakri said: And the Sheikh of our Sheikhs, the erudite scholar Sheikh Ahmad An-Nakhli, may God (Allah) the Exalted have mercy on him, said in his *Thabat*... Then after quoting from An-Nakhli some of what preceded regarding the virtue of this Hizb and that it protects from the evil of Jinn and mankind to the end of it, he said, may God (Allah) be pleased with him: I say: And here is something the reciter must be aware of, which is that he must beware of exposing himself to the people of spiritual attraction (Jadhb), saying, “I have read the Hizb, so none of them can harm me,” for God (Allah) the Exalted is protective, and He may take revenge for them even if they do not act on their own behalf. Likewise, he should beware of his Sheikh’s heart turning against him more than others, for it affects him, even if only by halting his spiritual journey. The people of the path have stipulated that the seeker in this path, when he enters the allegiance of a pleasing, excellent Sheikh of the path, no need is fulfilled for him except at his hands in this world and the Hereafter. And if the supplication of a parent leads to a veil (blocking), as narrated by Ibn Majah from Umm Hakim, the Sheikh is likewise, or even more so without doubt. And in the noble Hadith narrated by Ash-Shirazi from Ibn ‘Abbas, may God (Allah) the Exalted be pleased with them both, the interpreter of the Quran who reached his hope: “Whoever bestows a favor upon a people and they do not thank him for it, so he supplicates against them, it will be answered for him.” Know that among the men (Awliya’) are those who are like a double-edged sword, so beware of quarreling with him or touching him [with harm], even if you think you have two grandfathers in the coffin. And some of them, his bow is strung, his sword is drawn and unsheathed, his spear has a sharpened tip, and his horse is saddled and bridled, like our Sheikh, the Ash-gray Falcon, for he informed about that regarding himself and elaborated. And he said what means: “Two men opposed me in my state, so I struck their necks in the presence of God (Allah) the Exalted.” So beware of delusion and temptation. And it has come in the noble Hadith from the master of the children of ‘Adnan, may God (Allah) the Exalted grant us a cup of His pure love through him: “Whoever prays the dawn prayer is under the protection of God (Allah) the Exalted, so do not let God (Allah) pursue you for anything regarding His protection,” and in a narration from him, peace and blessings of God (Allah) the Exalted be upon him: “Whoever prays the dawn prayer is under the protection of God (Allah), and his reckoning is with God (Allah).” And whoever is under the protection of God (Allah), how can anyone from the people of God (Allah) attack him, unless it is with the intention of discipline and advice for the sake of God (Allah), or it is by permission or command from God (Allah) the Exalted? So understand, and may God (Allah) the Exalted grant us and you success to traverse His most upright path. My master Mustafa Al-Bakri, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, said after what he mentioned: As for our chain of transmission regarding this noble Hizb and the authored works of An-Nawawi, may God (Allah) the Exalted have mercy on him, the possessor of lofty status, I narrate it from our Sheikh, the knowing, practicing, virtuous, and perfect scholar, Sheikh Muhammad bin Ahmad Ad-Dimyati Ash-Shafi‘i, famously known as Ibn Al-Mayyit Al-Budayri, with his chain recorded in his *Thabat* which he authorized me with. Then he mentioned the expression of the *Thabat*, the summary of which is that his Sheikh Al-Budayri mentioned above narrates the books of An-Nawawi with his chain up to Al-Hafiz Ibn Hajar, from Al-Hafiz ‘Abd Ar-Rahim Al-‘Iraqi, from ‘Ala’ Ad-Din bin Al-‘Attar, from the Pole, the divine knower, the ascetic, the pious, Yahya Abu Zakariya An-Nawawi. Then Sayyid Al-Bakri said: We have a chain for this Hizb and the books of the author through the route of our Sheikh, Sheikh Muhammad Abu Al-Mawahib Al-Hanbali, from his late father Sheikh ‘Abd Al-Baqi Al-Hanbali Al-Ba‘li, and his chain is established in his *Thabat*. We also have a connection to the chain of Sheikh Ahmad An-Nakhli included in his *Thabat* through our Sheikh, Sheikh Muhammad bin Ibrahim Ad-Dakdakji. Sayyid Al-Bakri stated all of this in the introduction to the commentary on the aforementioned Hizb. Then he concluded the commentary with some of the previous expressions of Sheikh Ahmad An-Nakhli and said after the first formula of sending peace, which is his saying: “And may the prayers and peace of God (Allah) be upon our master Muhammad, and upon his family and companions,” before his saying: “Then he blows without spitting, then he says: I have hidden myself...” to the end of it. In many manuscripts, the addition “I have hidden myself...” is not found, but it is established according to what is in most of the sound manuscripts. Perhaps the author, may God (Allah) the Exalted have mercy on him, added it after the first version had spread, then the fame of the second version overtook it, and the sending of prayers on the first would have occurred at the end of the Hizb. The practice of the Nation (Ummah) has continued to mention the prayers and peace upon the Compassionate, the Merciful, at the beginning and end of every authored work, seeking blessings by the mention of his noble name, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him. Then Sayyid Al-Bakri said: And in some manuscripts of the wird, the prayer is placed first, and “I have hidden...” is placed last, after the blowing. In one manuscript, there is the addition of “The Chosen, the Honored (Al-Mustafa Al-Mukarram).” And in the narration of the perfect scholar ‘Abdullah bin Salim, there is no “I have hidden myself” in it, but it is established in others, as we presented. End of the words of my master Mustafa Al-Bakri.

## (Referencing the Remembrances and Prophetic Supplications found in the Fourth Fortress [Hisn])

(107) Al-Halabi said in *Hall Al-‘Iqal*: ‘Abdullah bin Aban Ath-Thaqafi narrated, saying: Al-Hajjaj bin Yusuf sent me in pursuit of Anas bin Malik, may God (Allah) be pleased with him. I thought he was hiding from me, so I came to him with my horsemen and foot soldiers. There he was, sitting at the door of his house, with his legs stretched out. I said: “Answer the Amir.” He said: “Which Amir?” I said: “Abu Muhammad Al-Hajjaj.” He said, uncaring about him: “God (Allah) has humiliated him. I only see him as arrogant, because the mighty is the one who derives might from the obedience of God (Allah) the Exalted, and the humiliated is the one who is humiliated by the disobedience of God (Allah) the Exalted. Your companion has transgressed, tyrannized, aggressed, and opposed the Book of God (Allah) and the Sunnah, and by God (Allah), God (Allah) will surely take revenge on him.” So I said to him: “Stop this talk and answer the Amir.” So he stood up with me until we presented ourselves before him. He said to him: “Are you Anas bin Malik?” He said: “Yes.” He said: “Do you know what I want to do with you?” He said: “No.” He said: “I want to kill you the worst of killings.” Anas said: “If I knew that was in your hand, I would have worshipped you besides God (Allah). Indeed, you have no way against me.” Al-Hajjaj said: “And why is that?” He said: “Because the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, taught me a supplication and said: ‘Whoever supplicates with it every morning, no one will have a way against him,’ and I have supplicated with it this morning of mine.” Al-Hajjaj said to him: “Teach it to me.” He said: “God (Allah) forbid that I teach it to anyone while you are alive.” Al-Hajjaj said: “Let him go.” Then, when death approached Anas, he taught the supplication to his brethren, and it is this: “In the name of God (Allah), The Entirely Merciful (ar-Rahman), The Especially Merciful (ar-Rahim). In the name of God (Allah), the best of names... up to his saying: by Your mercy, O Most Merciful of the merciful (ya Arham ar-Rahimin).” However, what is apparent is that the end of Anas’s supplication is his saying: “Indeed, my Lord (ar-Rabb) is upon a straight path,” and therefore I confined it to that here. As for what comes after it, from his saying: “O God (Allah), as You were subtle in Your greatness...” to the end of it, it is another supplication among those mentioned by the author of *Al-Ihya’* and others, and I have mentioned it in this book sequentially. Anas has another supplication in another story with Al-Hajjaj which I will mention shortly from *Al-Khasa’is Al-Kubra* by As-Suyuti.

(108) Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya extracted from Abu Hurayrah, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: “There is no might nor power except by God (Allah) is a cure for ninety-nine diseases, the easiest of which is the removal of worry.”

(109) Al-Khara’iti narrated from Ibn Mas‘ud, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, from the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, that he said: “If you fear something from anyone, say: ‘O God (Allah), The Lord (ar-Rabb) of the seven heavens and what is in them... up to his saying: and there is no might nor power except by God (Allah).’”

(110) Imam Ahmad and Abu Dawud narrated from Abu Musa, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, when he feared a people, used to say: “O God (Allah), we place You in their necks, and we seek refuge in You from their evils.”

(111) Al-Hafiz As-Suyuti said in *Al-Khasa’is Al-Kubra*: Ibn Sa‘d extracted from Aban bin ‘Ayyash that Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, spoke to Al-Hajjaj. So he said to him: “Were it not for your service to the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, and the letter of the Commander of the Faithful, there would have been a matter between me and you.” Anas said: “Far from it. When my nasal passage thickened and the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, could not recognize my voice, he taught me words with which the arrogance of no tyrant nor the stubbornness of any obstinate person will harm me, along with the facilitation of needs and meeting believers with love.” Al-Hajjaj said: “If only you would teach them to me.” He said: “You are not worthy of that.” So Al-Hajjaj sent two hundred thousand dirhams to him with his two sons and said to them: “Be gentle with the Sheikh, perhaps you will obtain the words.” But they did not obtain them. When it was three days before he died, he said: “Take these words and do not place them where they do not belong: God (Allah) is the Greatest, God (Allah) is the Greatest... up to his saying: and from above me, and from below me.”

(112) At-Tirmidhi narrated from Abu Hurayrah, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that when a matter distressed the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, he would raise his gaze to the sky and say: “Glory be to God (Allah), The Great (al-‘Azim).” And if he strove hard in supplication, he would say: “O Ever-Living (ya Hayy), O Sustainer (ya Qayyum).”

(113) Anas bin Malik, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, narrated that the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, passed by a man praying. When he finished the Tashahhud, he started saying: “O God (Allah), I ask You by the fact that to You belongs all praise... up to his saying: O Ever-Living (ya Hayy), O Sustainer (ya Qayyum).” So the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: “Do you know what he supplicated with?” We said: “God (Allah) and His Messenger know best.” He said: “He supplicated to God (Allah) by His Great Name which, if He is called by it, He answers, and if He is asked by it, He gives.” In most narrations, “O Compassionate (ya Hannan)” is not mentioned, and in some of them, “O Possessor of Majesty and Honor (ya Dha al-Jalal wa al-Ikram)” is not mentioned. Narrated by Abu Dawud, An-Nasa’i, and Al-Hakim, who said: Authentic according to the conditions of Muslim. And in another narration, “by His Greatest Name” instead of “The Great.”

(114) From Buraydah, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that he entered the mosque with the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, and there was a man praying, supplicating by saying: “O God (Allah), I ask You... up to his saying: and there is none equivalent to Him.” The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: “By Him in whose hand is my soul, he has asked God (Allah) by His Greatest Name which, if He is asked by it, He gives, and if He is called by it, He answers.” Extracted by Abu Dawud, At-Tirmidhi, An-Nasa’i, and Ibn Majah, and authenticated by Ibn Hibban and Al-Hakim.

(115) From Abu Ad-Darda’, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, led us in the ‘Asr prayer. A dog passed by, and before its front leg could reach its hind leg, it died. The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, turned and said: “Who was the one supplicating against this dog just now?” A man from the people said: “I was, O Messenger of God (Allah).” He said: “You supplicated to God (Allah) the Mighty and Majestic by His Name which, if He is called by it, He answers, and if He is asked by it, He gives. Even if you had supplicated for the nation of Muhammad, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, that they be forgiven, He would have done it. How did you supplicate?” He said: “I said: ‘O God (Allah), I ask You... up to his saying: O Possessor of Majesty and Honor (ya Dha al-Jalal wa al-Ikram), suffice us against this dog with whatever You will.’” And it did not take long before it died. Narrated by Abu Bakr Al-Qati‘i in his *Amali*.

(116) From Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that ‘A’ishah, may God (Allah) be pleased with her, said: “O Messenger of God (Allah), teach me the Great Name of God (Allah).” The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said to her: “Stand up, perform ablution, then supplicate so that I may hear.” She said: So I did, and I said: “O God (Allah), I ask You by Your Beautiful Names... up to her saying: by Your Great, Greatest Name.” The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: “You hit the mark, by Him in whose hand is my soul.” Narrated by At-Tabarani in *Kitab Ad-Du‘a*.

(117) From Anas, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: “If you have a need and you would love for it to succeed, say: ‘There is no deity except God (Allah), alone with no partner... up to his saying: O Most Merciful of the merciful (ya Arham ar-Rahimin).’” Narrated by At-Tabarani in his *Mu‘jam As-Saghir*, and At-Tirmidhi and Ibn Majah narrated similar to it from ‘Abdullah bin Abi Awfa.

(118) From Abu Talhah Al-Ansari, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: We were with the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, in a military campaign, and he met the enemy. I heard him saying: “O Master (Malik) of the Day of Judgment, It is You we worship and You we ask for help.” He said: So I saw the men falling down, being struck by the angels from in front of them and from behind them. Narrated by At-Tabarani in *Kitab Ad-Du‘a*.

(119) Abu Dawud, At-Tirmidhi, and Ibn Hibban narrated from ‘Uthman bin ‘Affan, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, from the Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, who said: “Whoever says when he reaches the evening: ‘In the name of God (Allah), with whose name nothing on earth or in heaven can harm, and He is The All-Hearing (as-Sami’), The All-Knowing (al-‘Alim)’ three times, no sudden affliction will strike him until morning comes. And whoever says it when he reaches the morning three times, no sudden affliction will strike him until evening comes.” In At-Tirmidhi’s narration: “nothing will harm him.” He said: A sound, authentic Hadith. At-Tirmidhi, Abu Dawud, and Ibn Majah also narrated, as is in *Al-Mishkat*, from Aban bin ‘Uthman who said: I heard my father say: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: “There is no servant who says in the morning of every day and the evening of every night: ‘In the name of God (Allah), with whose name nothing on earth or in heaven can harm, and He is The All-Hearing (as-Sami’), The All-Knowing (al-‘Alim)’ three times, except that nothing will harm him.” Aban had been struck by paralysis, so a man started looking at him. Aban said: “What are you looking at? The Hadith is exactly as I narrated it to you, but I did not say it that day so that God (Allah) could execute His decree.”

(120) At-Tirmidhi Al-Hakim said in *Nawadir Al-Usul* what follows: The one hundred and seventy-seventh principle regarding the words of relief, forgiveness, and prompting. From ‘Abdullah bin Ja‘far, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, used to say: “Prompt your dying ones with: There is no deity except God (Allah), The Forbearing (al-Halim), The Generous (al-Karim). Glory be to God (Allah), The Lord (ar-Rabb) of the seven heavens and The Lord (ar-Rabb) of the Great Throne. All praise is due to God (Allah), The Lord (ar-Rabb) of the worlds.” They said: “O Messenger of God (Allah), so how are they for the living?” He said: “Even better and better.” These words are called the "Words of Relief" by the People of the Household (Ahl al-Bayt); they supplicate with them during calamities and hardships. This is because of what was narrated from ‘Ali, may God (Allah) honor his face, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, said: “Shall I not teach you words which, if you say them, your sins will be forgiven, even though you are already forgiven? There is no deity except God (Allah), The Most High (al-‘Aliyy), The Great (al-‘Azim). There is no deity except God (Allah), The Forbearing (al-Halim), The Generous (al-Karim). Glory be to God (Allah), The Lord (ar-Rabb) of the heavens and The Lord (ar-Rabb) of the Great Throne. All praise is due to God (Allah), The Lord (ar-Rabb) of the worlds.”

(From what was reported from some of the Prophets, peace and blessings be upon them):

(121) ‘Abdullah the son of Imam Ahmad extracted in *Zawa’id Az-Zuhd* from Yahya bin Sulaym who said: While Musa, peace be upon him, was walking, a need occurred to him, so he said: “What God (Allah) wills (Ma sha' Allah),” and suddenly his need was right in front of him. He asked his Lord (ar-Rabb), so He revealed to him: “Did you not know that your saying ‘What God (Allah) wills’ is the most successful means by which you ask for needs?”

(146) “O God (Allah), send prayers upon our master Muhammad, the unlettered Prophet, the pure, the immaculate, a prayer by which knots are untied and distresses are relieved.” This prayer is the one hundred and twentieth of the prayers in *Sa‘adat Ad-Darayn*, and I mentioned regarding its virtue there that Sheikh Shihab Ad-Din Ahmad bin ‘Abd Al-Latif Ash-Sharji Az-Zabidi, the author of the summary of Al-Bukhari, mentioned in the book *As-Silat wa Al-‘Awa’id*, quoting from some of the righteous, that he said: “Whoever falls into distress and says: ‘O God (Allah), send prayers upon our master Muhammad, the unlettered Prophet...’ to the end of it, and repeats that, God (Allah) will relieve his distress.”

(147) “O God (Allah), send prayers upon our master Muhammad, a prayer by which You save us from all terrors and calamities...” to the end of it. It is the prayer famously known as *Al-Munjiyah* (The Saving Prayer), and it is tested for relieving distresses. It is the twenty-sixth in *Afdal As-Salawat*, and I quoted regarding its virtue from the commentary of *Ad-Dala’il* that whoever says it one thousand times for every important matter and affliction, God (Allah) will relieve him and he will attain his hope. It mentioned the story of the ship that was about to sink, and when they recited it by the command of the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, God (Allah) brought them relief. I expanded on the speech regarding its virtue there with numerous quotes, so review it if you wish.

(148) “O God (Allah), send a perfect prayer...” This is the relieving prayer (As-Salat At-Tafrijiyyah) belonging to Imam At-Tazi, and I do not know if he is ‘Abd Al-Wahhab At-Tazi, the Sheikh of my master Ahmad bin Idris and the successor of my master ‘Abd Al-‘Aziz Ad-Dabbagh, or someone else. It has a great secret in relieving distresses. I have mentioned much merit for it in *Afdal As-Salawat*. Know that the statement of Sheikh Muhammad Haqqi An-Nazili in *Khazinat Al-Asrar* that “it is called among the Moroccans As-Salat An-Naziyyah” is a corruption (tashif) of At-Taziyyah.

(149) “O God (Allah), send prayers, peace, and blessings upon our master Muhammad and upon the family of our master Muhammad. My means have run out; catch up to me (rescue me), O Messenger of God (Allah).” This prayer is tested for relieving distress. The Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, taught it in a dream to the Mufti of the Levant, Hamid Effendi Al-‘Imadi, when a certain vizier of Damascus wanted to assault him. When he recited it, God (Allah) the Exalted relieved his distress. Ibn ‘Abidin mentioned that he and others tested it, and God (Allah) relieved them. I have also tested it myself, and God (Allah) brought relief to me, and many brethren informed me that they tested it, and God (Allah) brought them relief.

(150) I met in noble Jerusalem in the year 1305 with the venerated saint, my master Sheikh Hasan Abu Halawa Al-Ghazzi, may God (Allah) have mercy on him, numerous times. He prayed for me and authorized me with the Qadiriyyah Path and with a formulation of prayer upon the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, for relieving distress. If the distressed person recites it much, God (Allah) brings him relief, and it is: “O God (Allah), send prayers upon our master Muhammad, the beloved, the dutiful, the healer of illnesses, and the reliever of distresses.” The reciter should add: “And upon his family and companions, and peace.”

(151) “O God (Allah), send prayers upon our master Muhammad, the opener, the good, the pure, the mercy of God (Allah) to the worlds, and upon his good and pure family, and send abundant peace.” It is the one hundred and fourth in *Sa‘adat Ad-Darayn*. I mentioned regarding its virtue there that he mentioned it in *Kunuz Al-Asrar* and said regarding its virtue that it is for relieving distress. My master ‘Abdullah bin As‘ad Al-Yafi‘i took it from his Sheikh, my master Abu Al-Mahasin Yusuf Al-Fasi, may God (Allah) benefit us by him.

(152) This is the *Hizb An-Nasr* (The Litany of Victory) of my master, the pole of the poles, the renowned and great Imam of the Awliya’, the greatest succor, my master ‘Abd Al-Qadir Al-Jilani, may God (Allah) sanctify his mighty secret and benefit us and the Muslims by him. I transcribed it from the collection of his Awrad named *Al-Fuyudat Ar-Rabbaniyyah fi Al-Ma’athir Al-Qadiriyyah*, and I corrected it against another manuscript written in old handwriting within a collection of his works, may God (Allah) be pleased with him.

The Conclusion: On ten important, tested benefits for relief and fulfilling needs.

(The First Benefit) He said in *Hall Al-‘Iqal*: Al-Hakim extracted in *Al-Mustadrak* from ‘Abdullah bin Abi Awfa, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God (Allah), peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, came out to us and sat down, then said: “Whoever has a need from God (Allah) or from any of the children of Adam, let him perform ablution, and let him perform it well, then let him pray two rak‘ahs. Then let him praise God (Allah) the Exalted and send prayers upon the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, and let him say: ‘There is no deity except God (Allah), The Forbearing (al-Halim), The Generous (al-Karim). Glory be to God (Allah), The Lord (ar-Rabb) of the Great Throne. All praise is due to God (Allah), The Lord (ar-Rabb) of the worlds. I ask You for the causes of Your mercy, the resolves of Your forgiveness, protection from every sin, and safety from every offense. Do not leave for me a sin except that You forgive it, nor a worry except that You relieve it, nor a need that is pleasing to You except that You fulfill it, O Most Merciful of the merciful (ya Arham ar-Rahimin).’”

(The Second Benefit) Al-Hafiz As-Suyuti said in the book *Al-Araj fi Ad‘iyat Al-Faraj*: Abu Dawud, An-Nasa’i, Ibn Majah, and Ibn Abi Ad-Dunya extracted from Ibn ‘Abbas, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, from the Prophet, peace and blessings of God (Allah) be upon him, that he said: “Whoever constantly seeks forgiveness (Istighfar), God (Allah) will make for him a relief from every worry, a way out from every tightness, and will provide for him from where he does not expect.”

(The Third Benefit) He said in *Hall Al-‘Iqal*: It is narrated from ‘Ali bin Al-Husayn, may God (Allah) be pleased with them both, that he used to say: “O my sons, if a calamity afflicts you in the world, or poverty or a severe matter befalls you, let the man among you perform his ablution for prayer, and let him pray four rak‘ahs or two rak‘ahs. When he finishes his prayer, let him say: ‘O Destination of every complaint, O Hearer of every secret counsel, O Healer of every affliction, O Knower of every hidden thing, O Remover of whatever He wills of calamity, O Savior of Musa, O Chooser of Muhammad, O Friend of Ibrahim, I supplicate to You with the supplication of one whose need is severe, whose strength is weak, and whose means are exhausted; the supplication of the poor stranger who finds no one to remove what he is in except You, O Most Merciful of the merciful (ya Arham ar-Rahimin). There is no deity except You; glory be to You. Indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers.’”

(The Fourth Benefit) As-Suyuti said in his book *Siham Al-Isabah fi Ad-Da‘wat Al-Mustajabah*: Abu Nu‘aym extracted in *Al-Hilyah* from Ma‘ruf Al-Karkhi who said: Whoever says when he wakes up turning in his bed: “Glory be to God (Allah), all praise is due to God (Allah), there is no deity except God (Allah), and I seek the forgiveness of God (Allah). O God (Allah), I ask You from Your bounty and Your mercy, for they are in Your hand, no one possesses them other than You,” God (Allah) will say to Jibril, who is entrusted with fulfilling the needs of the servants: “O Jibril, fulfill the need of My servant.”

(The Fifth Benefit) He said in *Hall Al-‘Iqal*: Abu Al-‘Abbas Ahmad Ash-Sharji, who is Imam Az-Zabidi, the author of the summary of Al-Bukhari, mentioned in his book *Al-Fawa’id* what follows: From the secret of the Awliya’ and their guidance for anyone whom a matter concerns or a distress befalls, is that he performs ablution and prays the sunset (Maghrib) prayer on the eve of Friday (Thursday night). Then he secludes himself in prayer and the remembrance of God (Allah) the Exalted, and he does not speak to anyone until he prays the night (Isha) prayer. When he prays the Witr, he says in the last prostration of the Witr: “O God (Allah), O Lord (ya Rabb), O Entirely Merciful (ya Rahman), O Ever-Living (ya Hayy), O Sustainer (ya Qayyum), in You I seek aid, O God (Allah).” He says that one hundred times, then asks for his need, and he must avoid supplicating against a Muslim.

(The Sixth Benefit) The Imam, the knower of God (Allah), Abu Al-Mu’ayyad Muhammad bin Khatir Ad-Din, said in the second jewel of his book *Al-Jawahir Al-Khams*, which he authored in the year 949—and it is an amazing book with no equal in its field—that for needs, one reads this verse one thousand and one times in a state of purity, and he does not speak to anyone during the recitation. Whoever does that, God (Allah) will fulfill his need, whatever need it may be from the needs of this world and the Hereafter. And this is the verse: {In the name of God (Allah), The Entirely Merciful (ar-Rahman), The Especially Merciful (ar-Rahim). And whoever fears God (Allah) - He will make for him a way out, and will provide for him from where he does not expect. And whoever relies upon God (Allah) - then He is sufficient for him. Indeed, God (Allah) will accomplish His purpose. God (Allah) has already set for everything a [decreed] extent.}

(The Seventh Benefit) He said in the book *Al-Jawahir Al-Khams* as well: It is narrated from Imam Muqatil, may God (Allah) be pleased with him, that he said: Whoever has a need from The Possessor of Majesty and Bestowal (Dhu al-Jalal wa al-Ifdal), let him read this supplication on the eve of Friday (Thursday night) one hundred times: “There is no deity except God (Allah), The Most High (al-‘Aliyy), The Great (al-‘Azim). There is no deity except God (Allah), The Forbearing (al-Halim), The Generous (al-Karim). Glory be to God (Allah), The Lord (ar-Rabb) of the seven heavens and The Lord (ar-Rabb) of the Great Throne. There is no deity except God (Allah), The King (al-Malik), The Truth (al-Haqq), The Clear (al-Mubin). There is no deity except God (Allah), my Lord (ar-Rabb) and The Lord (ar-Rabb) of my first forefathers. Glory be to God (Allah), all praise is due to God (Allah), there is no deity except God (Allah), and God (Allah) is the Greatest. And there is no might nor power except by God (Allah), The Most High (al-‘Aliyy), The Great (al-‘Azim). O Compassionate (ya Hannan), O Bestower (ya Mannan), O Ever-Living (ya Hayy), O Sustainer (ya Qayyum), O Possessor of Majesty and Honor (ya Dhu al-Jalal wa al-Ikram). By Your mercy I seek aid, and may the prayers of God (Allah) be upon our master Muhammad and his entire family.”

(The Eighth Benefit) He said in *Hall Al-‘Iqal*: And from what we received from the majestic Sheikhs and what we advised most of our brethren and close friends with, is that the imprisoned and the captive reads in one sitting, with a pure intention and presence of heart, facing the Qiblah, Surah Yusuf, peace be upon him. Then he says one thousand times: “In the name of God (Allah), The Entirely Merciful (ar-Rahman), The Especially Merciful (ar-Rahim), and there is no might nor power except by God (Allah), The Most High (al-‘Aliyy), The Great (al-‘Azim). What God (Allah) wills will be, and God (Allah) is sufficient for us, and He is the best Disposer of affairs.” Then he says: “O God (Allah), O Subtle One (ya Latif), O Subtle One (ya Latif), O Subtle One (ya Latif), O He whose subtlety encompasses the people of the heavens and the earths, I ask You, O God (Allah), to be subtle with me with Your hidden, unseen subtlety which, if You are subtle with it towards anyone from Your creation, he is sufficed. For You have said, and Your word is the truth: God (Allah) is Subtle (Latif) with His servants; He provides for whom He wills, and He is The Strong (al-Qawiyy), The Mighty (al-‘Aziz).” For God (Allah) will deliver him expeditiously and bring him relief immediately, by the permission of God (Allah) the Exalted. This has been tested and proved true. End quote.

(The Ninth Benefit) As-Suyuti said in the book *Al-Araj*: Ibn Bashkuwal narrated in the book *Al-Mustaghithin Billah* from ‘Abdullah bin Al-Mubarak who said: I went out for Jihad, and with me was a horse. While I was on the road, the horse fell down. A man with a handsome face and a pleasant scent passed by me and said: “Would you like to ride your horse?” I said: “Yes.” So he placed his hand on the horse's forehead until he reached its nostril, and said: “I adjure you, O illness, by the might of the Might of God (Allah), and by the greatness of the Greatness of God (Allah), and by the majesty of the Majesty of God (Allah), and by the power of the Power of God (Allah), and by the authority of the Authority of God (Allah), and by 'There is no deity except God (Allah)', and by what the pen flowed with from God (Allah), and by 'There is no might nor power except by God (Allah)', that you depart.” He said: So the horse shook itself, and the man took my stirrup and said: “Mount.” So I mounted and caught up with my companions. When it was the morning of the next day and the enemy appeared, there he was in front of us. I said: “Are you not my companion from yesterday?” He said: “Yes.” I said: “I ask you by God (Allah), who are you?” He sprang up standing, and the earth shook beneath him, turning green, and there he was, Al-Khidr, peace be upon him. Ibn Al-Mubarak said: “I never said these words over a sick entity except that it was healed by the permission of God (Allah) the Exalted.”

(The Tenth Benefit) My master, the knower of God (Allah), Sayyid Mustafa Al-Bakri, said towards the end of his commentary on the Hizb of Imam An-Nawawi, regarding His saying, Exalted is He: {Muhammad is the Messenger of God (Allah)} to the end of the Surah: “Whoever is granted success by God (Allah) to write the verse and carry it with him will witness wonders in acceptance, subjugation, the facilitation of every difficulty, and the attainment of all goals. And someone whose transmission is verifiably sound mentioned to me, saying: ‘I found it (its benefit) for someone who had difficulty achieving a desired goal. And I cast it upon a beast, so it became submissive and compliant after it had been unruly. And through it, a great number of people whom I cannot count were cured of a cold fever.’” Then he said: “And this verse is for growth, blessing, intensity, strength, and guarding against every affliction for men and children. And in the *Fawa’id* of As-Sanusi, may God (Allah) the Exalted have mercy on him, is that whoever writes the verse {Then He sent down upon you...} and the verse {Muhammad is the Messenger of God (Allah)} and hangs them on himself, he will be treated with subtlety in all his affairs, God (Allah) the Exalted will grant him victory over his enemies, and will relieve him of every worry and grief. They also benefit for outward and inward ailments as an ointment and a drink; they are written in a clean vessel and washed off with rose oil and olive oil, and it is applied to every pain such as warts, abscesses, and bloating, and that will disappear shortly. It is tested and true.” End quote.
