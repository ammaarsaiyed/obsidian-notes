## The First Fortress of Relief from Sorrows and Joy for the Hearts

  
(1) In the name of Allah (Allah), the Most Gracious (al-Rahman), the Most Merciful (al-Rahim). And there is no power and no strength except by Allah (Allah), the Most High, the Most Great.

(2) O Allah (Allah), I ask You by that to You belongs all praise; there is no god but You, the Originator (Badi') of the heavens and the earth, O Possessor of Majesty and Honor (Dhu al-Jalal wa-al-Ikram), O Ever-Living (al-Hayy), O Sustainer (al-Qayyum).

(3) O Allah (Allah), I ask You by that to You belongs all praise; there is no god but You, the Compassionate (al-Hannan), the Bestower (al-Mannan), the Originator (Badi') of the heavens and the earth, O Possessor of Majesty and Honor (Dhu al-Jalal wa-al-Ikram), I ask You for Paradise and I seek refuge in You from the Fire.

(4) O Allah (Allah), I ask You, there is no god but You, Lord (Rabb) of the seven heavens and Lord (Rabb) of the Mighty Throne, and I ask You, there is no god but You, Lord (Rabb) of the seven heavens and Lord (Rabb) of the Noble Throne, and I ask You, there is no god but You, Lord (Rabb) of the seven heavens and the seven earths and whatever is in them, that You are over all things powerful. (Then he asks for his need.)

(5) O Lord (Rabb), O Lord (Rabb), O Lord (Rabb), O Lord (Rabb).

(6) O Originator (Badi') of the heavens, O Ever-Living (al-Hayy), O Sustainer (al-Qayyum).

(7) O Allah (Allah), I ask You for all good, what I know of it and what I do not know, and I ask You by Your Great Name, which if You are called upon by it You answer, and if You are asked by it You give. (There is no god but Allah (Allah), and Allah (Allah) is the Greatest. There is no god but Allah (Allah), alone without partner. To Him belongs the dominion and to Him belongs the praise, and He is over all things powerful. There is no god but Allah (Allah), and there is no power and no strength except by Allah (Allah).)

(8) There is no god but Allah (Allah), and Allah (Allah) is the Greatest. There is no god but Allah (Allah), alone without partner. To Him belongs the dominion and to Him belongs the praise, and He is over all things powerful. There is no god but Allah (Allah), and there is no power and no strength except by Allah (Allah).

(9) O Allah (Allah), I am Your servant, son of Your servant, son of Your maidservant; my forelock is in Your hand, Your judgment is carried out upon me, Your decree over me is just. I ask You by every name that is Yours, with which You named Yourself, or sent it down in Your Book, or taught it to anyone of Your creation, or kept it to Yourself in the knowledge of the unseen with You, that You make the Qur'an the spring of my heart, the light of my sight, the removal of my sorrow, and the departure of my worry.

(10) Allah (Allah) is sufficient for me against the servants; the Creator (al-Khaliq) is sufficient for me against the created; the Provider (al-Razzaq) is sufficient for me against those provided for; sufficient for me is He who is sufficient for me; Allah (Allah) is sufficient for me, and He is the best Disposer of affairs. Allah (Allah) is sufficient for me; there is no god but He; upon Him I have relied, and He is the Lord (Rabb) of the Mighty Throne.

(11) I have put my trust in the Living (al-Hayy) who does not die, and praise be to Allah who has not taken a son, and who has no partner in sovereignty, and who has no protector out of weakness, and magnify Him greatly.

(12) Allah is sufficient for me, and how excellent a disposer of affairs.

(13) Allah is sufficient for us, and how excellent a disposer of affairs.

(14) O One who exists before everything, and O One who forms everything, and O One who exists after everything, do such-and-such to me.

(15) You were and You are, and You are Living (al-Hayy), who does not die; the eyes sleep and the stars grow dim, and You are Living, Self-Sustaining (al-Qayyum); neither slumber nor sleep overtakes You, O Living (al-Hayy), O Self-Sustaining (al-Qayyum).

(16) There is no god but Allah, the Magnificent (al-‘Azim), the Forbearing (al-Halim). There is no god but Allah, Lord of the Mighty Throne. There is no god but Allah, Lord of the heavens and Lord of the earth, Lord of the Noble Throne.

(17) There is no god but Allah, the Forbearing (al-Halim), the Generous (al-Karim). Glory be to Allah, Lord of the seven heavens and Lord of the Mighty Throne. There is no god but You; Your neighbor is mighty, and Your praise is majestic, and there is no god besides You.

(18) There is no god but Allah, the Most High (al-‘Ali), the Forbearing (al-Halim). There is no god but Allah, Lord of the Mighty Throne. There is no god but Allah, Lord of the seven heavens and Lord of the Noble Throne. *

(19) O Allah, to You I complain of my weakness, the scarcity of my means, and my lowliness before people. You are the Most Merciful of the merciful (Arham al-Rahimin). To whom do You leave me? To an enemy who scowls at me, or to a relative over whom You have given control of my affair? If You are not angry with me, then I do not care; however, Your well-being is wider for me. I seek refuge in the light of Your Face by which the darknesses have shone, and by which the affair of this world and the hereafter has become right, lest Your wrath descend upon me or Your displeasure befall me. To You belongs the excuse until You are pleased, and there is no might and no power except by You.

(20) O Most Merciful of the merciful (Arham al-Rahimin) "three times"
